        <p>Usually my mom dries the clothes on the terrace and whenever I am at home she keeps asking me to look outside and tell her if it raining or not. Literally every half an hour or every time she hears a swoosh or a woosh from outside.</p> 
        <p>I was fed up and wanted to eliminate the middle man (<em>me</em>). So I came up with an app. It is called “Is it raining Arvind?” (<em>LOL I know</em>) . All the app does is tell my mom if it is raining outside now. And&nbsp;when it starts raining, the app triggers a buzzer and sends an SMS to my mom. Smart right! And I was off the hook.</p> 
        <p>So, this is how the app works. It uses the conductive property of the water. There are 2 wires that are connected to the Pi between a GPIO pin and 5v. And when these 2 pins are connected, it triggers a High on GPIO pin. So, we place these 2 wires outside on the terrace inside a bowl. Once the bowl is filled with water because of the rain, the circuit is completed and a High is triggered on the GPIO.</p> 
        <p>We take advantage of the GPIO high and then trigger the buzzer and send an SMS using Twilio. Smart right!?</p> 
        <p>Below is a quick demo of the app</p> 
        <p style="text-align: center;"> 
         <!-- iframe plugin v.4.2 wordpress.org/plugins/iframe/ --> <iframe width="640" height="360" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/TIQIXeDt5K4?rel=0" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen scrolling="yes" class="iframe-class"></iframe> </p> 
        <p>Sweet right!</p> 
        <p>You can find the completed code <a href="https://github.com/arvindr21/pi_checkRain" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'https://github.com/arvindr21/pi_checkRain', 'here');" title="Code" target="_blank">here</a>.</p> 
        <p>So, let us get started.</p>

        <h2>Prerequisites</h2> 
        <p>If you are new to Raspberry pi and have not yet installed Node.js on it, I would recommend going through <a title="Getting Started with Raspberry pi and Node.js" href="http://thejackalofjavascript.com/getting-started-raspberry-pi-node-js/" target="_blank">Getting Started with Raspberry pi and Node.js</a>.</p> 
        <p>If you are new to electronics devices and circuits, I would recommend going through the&nbsp;<a href="http://www.allaboutcircuits.com/videos/index.html" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://www.allaboutcircuits.com/videos/index.html', 'video lectures');" title="Video Lectures" target="_blank">video lectures</a> from All About Circuits.</p> 
        <p>If you are new to Twilio, refer <a title="Twilio" href="http://thejackalofjavascript.com/?s=twilio" target="_blank">this</a>.</p> 
        <p>Also do check out <a href="http://forum.allaboutcircuits.com/threads/resistance-of-water.18893/" onclick="__gaTracker('send', 'event', 'outbound-article', 'http://forum.allaboutcircuits.com/threads/resistance-of-water.18893/', 'Resistance of water');" title="Resistance of water" target="_blank">Resistance of water</a>.</p> 
        <h3>Components needed</h3> 
        <ol> 
         <li>1 – Raspberry pi B+</li> 
         <li>1 – Breadboard</li>

         <li>4 – Jumper wires</li> 
         <li>Some rain</li> 
        </ol>

        <h2>Setup project</h2> 
        <p>Now that we have a basic idea of what we are going to build, let us get started. First, we will setup the circuit.</p> 
        <p><a href="http://i1.wp.com/thejackalofjavascript.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Screen-Shot-2014-11-23-at-6.07.39-pm.png" data-rel="lightbox-0" title=""><img class="size-medium wp-image-2524 aligncenter" src="http://i0.wp.com/thejackalofjavascript.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" data-lazy-src="http://i1.wp.com/thejackalofjavascript.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Screen-Shot-2014-11-23-at-6.07.39-pm.png?resize=451%2C207" alt="Screen Shot 2014-11-23 at 6.07.39 pm" data-recalc-dims="1">

          </noscript></a></p> 
        <p>We will connect the positive end of the buzzer to GPIO pin 18 and the negative end to GND PIN 06 of the Pi. We will connect&nbsp;the 2 probes&nbsp;that are placed inside the bowl to GPIO PIN 17 and 5V PIN 02 of the PI.</p> 
        <p>This completes our circuit. To quickly test the circuit, take a bowl of water and dip the 2 probes and you should hear the buzzer “buzz”.</p> 
        <p><strong>DO NOT</strong> connect them directly. This <strong>WILL</strong> damage the GPIO pin. If you want to connect the 2 pin directly and test, you need a voltage divider circuit, that will take the load off the GPIO pin.</p> 
        <p><a href="http://i2.wp.com/thejackalofjavascript.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Screen-Shot-2014-11-23-at-6.19.08-pm.png" data-rel="lightbox-1" title=""><img class="size-medium wp-image-2525 aligncenter" src="http://i0.wp.com/thejackalofjavascript.com/wp-content/plugins/lazy-load/images/1x1.trans.gif" data-lazy-src="http://i2.wp.com/thejackalofjavascript.com/wp-content/uploads/2014/11/Screen-Shot-2014-11-23-at-6.19.08-pm.png?resize=451%2C204" alt="Screen Shot 2014-11-23 at 6.19.08 pm" data-recalc-dims="1">

          </noscript></a></p>

        <p>Lets get started with the Node.js program.</p> 
        <p>Login to your pi&nbsp;via ssh – terminal/putty. As soon as you ssh into pi, you will be landing inside the <em>/home/pi</em> folder. We will create a new folder here named&nbsp;<em>node_programs.</em> And inside this folder, we will be maintaining&nbsp;all our programs. Run</p> 
        <p style="text-align: center;"> <span id="crayon-59ba6afe3a76f909368806" class="crayon-syntax crayon-syntax-inline  crayon-theme-arduino-ide crayon-theme-arduino-ide-inline crayon-font-droid-sans-mono" style="font-size: 12px !important; line-height: 15px !important;font-size: 12px !important;"><span class="crayon-pre crayon-code" style="font-size: 12px !important; line-height: 15px !important;font-size: 12px !important; -moz-tab-size:4; -o-tab-size:4; -webkit-tab-size:4; tab-size:4;"><span class="crayon-e">mkdir </span><span class="crayon-v">node_programs</span></span></span></p> 
        <p>To step inside that folder, run</p> 
        <p style="text-align: center;"> <span id="crayon-59ba6afe3a784192398800" class="crayon-syntax crayon-syntax-inline  crayon-theme-arduino-ide crayon-theme-arduino-ide-inline crayon-font-droid-sans-mono" style="font-size: 12px !important; line-height: 15px !important;font-size: 12px !important;"><span class="crayon-pre crayon-code" style="font-size: 12px !important; line-height: 15px !important;font-size: 12px !important; -moz-tab-size:4; -o-tab-size:4; -webkit-tab-size:4; tab-size:4;"><span class="crayon-e">cd </span><span class="crayon-v">node_programs</span></span></span></p> 
        <p>&nbsp;For this post, we will create a new folder named&nbsp;<em>checkRain&nbsp;</em>and will step inside this folder. Run</p> 
        <p style="text-align: center;"> <span id="crayon-59ba6afe3a78b201019487" class="crayon-syntax crayon-syntax-inline  crayon-theme-arduino-ide crayon-theme-arduino-ide-inline crayon-font-droid-sans-mono" style="font-size: 12px !important; line-height: 15px !important;font-size: 12px !important;"><span class="crayon-pre crayon-code" style="font-size: 12px !important; line-height: 15px !important;font-size: 12px !important; -moz-tab-size:4; -o-tab-size:4; -webkit-tab-size:4; tab-size:4;"><span class="crayon-e">mkdir </span><span class="crayon-i">checkRain</span>&nbsp;<span class="crayon-o">&amp;&amp;</span><span class="crayon-h"> </span><span class="crayon-i">cd</span>&nbsp;<span class="crayon-v">checkRain</span></span></span></p>

}
}
});
