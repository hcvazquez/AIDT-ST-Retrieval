         <p>There are Word documents that you can edit, and then there are PDFs.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>PDFs are great because, like a printed piece of paper, they look the exact same, everywhere, no matter what app you view them with or what fonts you have installed. When you want to share a file and make sure it always looks the exact same, PDF is the way to go.</p> 
         <p>And yet, PDFs are some of the most difficult files to edit. Everyone has an app to preview PDFs, as viewers are built into most modern operating systems, but editing them typically requires Adobe Acrobat. Its a great tool if you own it, but its price tag makes it out of reach for many.</p> 
         <p>Thats OK though, because your recent copy of Word can edit PDF documents! In this tutorial, learn how to import and convert PDFs into Word format, so you can readily edit them.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>There are some formatting issues to watch out for when opening PDF files in Word, which we'll review. Also, if you have an older version of Word, there are some free and premium tools available to get your PDFs into a format that you can work with.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>Let's dive in and see how this works.&nbsp;<br></p> 
         <h2>How to Import, Convert, &amp; Edit PDFs in Word<br> </h2> 
         <p>One of the features included in later versions of Wordthe copy of Word you get with <a href="http://office.microsoft.com/home-premium/">Office 365</a> on a PCis built-in support for <a href="http://office.microsoft.com/en-001/word-help/edit-pdf-content-in-word-HA102903948.aspx">importing PDFs</a> and editing them in Word. You wont be editing the original PDF though. Instead, Word converts the PDF to Word's native format, turning the section headers to Word styles and more so you can easily edit and reuse the document.</p> 
         <p>To import your PDF into Word, just open it inside of Word or right-click on a PDF file in Explorer and select to open it in Word. Youll see a warning that the converted document will not be exactly the same as the original. Click <b>OK</b>, and after a brief pause your PDF will open in Word.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Word PDF Import" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20478/image/Edit-Pdf%20(1).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Imported 
           <a href="https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/i1099gi.pdf" target="_self">PDF document</a> shown in Word, with the TOC misaligned.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>Word does a great job importing PDF documents in some ways, but messes them up terribly in other ways. If the PDF you converted used fonts that are standard on PCs, your converted Word document may actually be quite similar to the original PDF; otherwise, you'll likely have oversized headers, oddly spaced paragraphs, and more.</p>
         <h3>How to Fix Word Text Styling in Converted PDFs&nbsp;</h3>
         <p>That can be relatively easily fixed, though, since Word imports the PDF text sections using Word Styles. Select a messed-up section to see what style it's using, then edit the style in Word and all similar sections will automatically update.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>Similarly, Word will import headers and footers correctlyeven recognizing when documents use different footers for opposite pagesso you can edit them once and the changes will show up across the entire document.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Word Document sections in an imported PDF" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20478/image/Edit-Pdf%20(2).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Edit the footers and headers of the PDF document the same as you would any other Word file.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <h3>When Word is Likely to Fail at Converting Your PDFs&nbsp;</h3>
         <p>While Word does a good job with imported PDFs filled with text and simple formatting, it tends to mess things up when you import PDF forms.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>It won't include any data you've saved in the form text fields, and will mess up spacing, lines, text boxes, and more in the import. For most purposes, you'd be better off recreating the form than importing it via Word.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="A PDF form imported into Word with poor formatting" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20478/image/Edit-Pdf%20(3).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           PDF forms fare the worst in a Word import.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <h2>Converting PDF to Word Format Using Other Popular Apps &nbsp;&nbsp;</h2>
         <p>Word for Mac, <a href="http://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/getting-started-with-microsoft-office-for-ipad--cms-19777">iPad</a>, and <a href="http://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/how-to-get-microsoft-office-for-free--cms-19778">Word Online</a>, along with older versions of Word on PCs, cannot open and edit PDFs. If youd like to edit PDFs in any of them, youll first need to convert them to Word format, and then open the converted file in Word.</p>
         <h3>PDF to Word Conversion Tools: Acrobat, Google Drive, and PDFPen</h3> 
         <p>If you have a copy of Adobe Acrobat (included with most Creative Suite and Creative Cloud subscriptions), you can use it to <a href="https://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/how-to-ocr-text-in-pdf-and-image-files-in-adobe-acrobat--cms-20406">convert PDF documents to Word format</a> on your Mac or PC. Its conversions are <em>very</em> high quality, and if your PDF document includes pictures with text, it will OCR the text so you can edit it as well. You may also be able to convert PDF documents in Google Drive:&nbsp;</p>
         <ul class="roundup-block__contents posts--half-width roundup-block--list">
          <li class="roundup-block__content"><a class="roundup-block__content-link" href="http://business.tutsplus.com/tutorials/how-to-ocr-documents-for-free-in-google-drive--cms-20460"><img class="roundup-block__preview-image" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/26/posts/20460/preview_image/docs_ocr.jpg">
            <div class="roundup-block__primary-category topic-business">
             Google Docs
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__content-title">
             How to OCR Documents for Free in Google Drive
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__author">
             Rebecca Tarnopol
            </div></a></li>
         </ul>
         <p>You can also use <a href="https://www.acrobat.com/welcome/en/home.html">Acrobat Online</a> to convert and OCR PDFs, although it's not free. If you need the best PDF to Word conversion, though, the cost is worth it. If youve scanned a document and want to convert it to Word format, its one of the few tools with consistently usable results.</p> 
         <p>If youre using a Mac and have a copy of <a href="http://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/how-to-perfect-a-pdf-with-pdfpen--mac-31675">PDFPen</a>, it can export PDFs to Word as well, complete with OCR. It includes many of Acrobats great editing and export features, so it's a good alternative option if you need to frequently export PDFs to Word.</p>
         <h3>Nitro PDFs to Word Format Conversion Tool</h3>
         <p>Another good option is a web app: <a href="https://www.nitrocloud.com">Nitro</a>. Its perfect whether you have one-off conversion needs or perform PDF to Word conversions more frequently. </p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Nitro Cloud converts PDF files to Word text" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20478/image/Edit-Pdf%20(4).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Nitro: another PDF to Word Conversion tool.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <h4>How to Use Nitro to Convert PDFs to Word Format</h4>
         <p>Just create an account, then upload the PDF you want to convert directly from your computer or Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive accounts. Then, click the <strong>Convert to Word</strong> button.&nbsp;<br></p>
         <p>After a brief pause for it to convert the document, you can download it and open it in Word on your PC or Mac or in <a href="https://office.live.com/start/Word.aspx">Word Online</a>, or in a Word alternative such as Pages or Google Docs.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="A converted PDF document from Nitro" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20478/image/Edit-Pdf%20(5).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Nitro's PDF conversion is quite nice.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <h4>How Useful is Nitro at Converting PDFs to Word Format?</h4>
         <p>Nitros PDF to word conversion isnt as perfect as Acrobats, but its far better than the built-in conversion in Word. Youll still have footers and heading styles brought over as in Word, but generally closer to the original document. There may be random font and spacing oddities, but overall, the converted document will be quite nice. </p>
         <p>Forms are especially surprising, since Nitro converts them almost perfectly. You can quite easily get a Word document out of most forms this way and, with minimal editing, get them looking the same as the original document.</p>
         <h2>How to Turn Your Document Back Into a PDF</h2>
         <p>No matter which version of Word you use to edit your PDF, your finished document will be a Word file by default. If you want to save your edited document as a PDF again, it's easy to do that as well.</p>
         <p>On a PC, just click the&nbsp;<b>File </b>tab&nbsp;and select the <b>Save As</b> option, then choose&nbsp;<b>PDF</b> and save the file with the file name you want. Click the <strong>Save</strong> button. <em>(Alternately, you could use the </em><strong><em>Print </em></strong><em>option and select </em><strong><em>Microsoft print to PDF</em></strong><em> as your printer.)</em></p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Changing a converted PDF file back to PDF in Word" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20478/image/Edit-Pdf%20(6).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           You can convert a Word document back to PDF format.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>In <a href="https://office.live.com/start/word.aspx" target="_self">Word Online</a>, click the&nbsp;<b>File</b> option, choose&nbsp;<b>Print</b>, then save the PDF it creates to your computer instead of printing it. On a Mac, click&nbsp;<b>File</b> &gt;&nbsp;<b>Save As...</b>, then in the Format drop-down select <b>Download as PDF</b>, and add the name you'd like.</p> 
         <h2>Conclusion: You Can Change Your PDF to Word Format Quickly</h2> 
         <p>PDF documents dont have to be the impenetrable documents they seem at first. Words built-in PDF import tool makes it as simple to edit a PDF as with any other document. You wont have the document fidelity PDFs are prided for, but you <em>will</em> be able to edit them without having to buy another expensive app.</p>
