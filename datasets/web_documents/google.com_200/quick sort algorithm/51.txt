           <p name="8a68" id="8a68" class="graf graf--p graf-after--figure">Technical front-end interviews are difficult. That’s a fact. Not only do you need to have a solid grasp of computer science fundamentals, but also an understanding of things like web performance, build systems, and CSS layout engines.</p>
           <p name="8176" id="8176" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">While there are resources out there, I’ve found that there are only a few <em class="markup--em markup--p-em">complete</em> guides for helping you prepare for a front-end interview. So I decided to write a topic by topic outline that will hopefully help you ace your next interview.</p>
           <h4 name="bfbf" id="bfbf" class="graf graf--h4 graf-after--p">Before the interview</h4>
           <p name="675c" id="675c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4">So before your interview, ask your recruiter for the format of the interview. Some interviews might revolve around a whiteboard while others might use an online text editor like CoderPad. It’s important to know so you can practice in the environment that your interview is going to take place in.</p>
           <p name="dc3e" id="dc3e" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Also, ask your recruiter for tips on what topics to focus on when preparing. The reason behind this is because in addition to front-end specific question, some companies will ask you traditional computer science questions about topics like searching and sorting algorithms.</p>
