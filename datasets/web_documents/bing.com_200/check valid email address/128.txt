                    <span class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">How do you know if an email address exists or not? The easy option would be that you send a dummy mail to that email address, wait for an hour or so and if your message bounces, it is very likely that the particular email address does not exist. The approach works but wouldnt it be nice if you could check any email address instantly without even sending that test message?</p><p class="qtext_para">The other slightly technical option to<span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://goo.gl/N9zX51" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 48717589);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="goo.gl"> verify an email address</a></span> is by querying the mail server. You connect to the mail server through telnet , enter your email address and the other email address that you are trying to verify. If the server response is an error code, the email address is probably not valid.</p><p class="qtext_para"><b>HOW TO CHECK EMAIL ADDRESSES INSTANTLY</b></p><p class="qtext_para">Let me share an extremely simple method for checking if an email address is valid and exists or not.</p><p class="qtext_para">Go to the login page of the email service and pretend that you no longer remember the password of your email account. The service will ask for your email address where they can send the password recovery instructions. Here if you enter an email address that does exist, the service is mostly likely to tell you that the particular user name does not exist. I have tested this with Google Apps, Yahoo Mail and Outlook (Hotmail) and the method works with them all.</p>
                     <div class="qtext_image_wrapper">
                      <canvas class="qtext_image_placeholder landscape qtext_image zoomable_in zoomable_in_feed" width="602.0" height="434.01869158878503"></canvas>
                      <img class="landscape qtext_image zoomable_in zoomable_in_feed lazy_loaded lazy_loading" src="" data-src="https://qph.ec.quoracdn.net/main-qimg-7e6b4902a6a86fc76e27f6963bd29d5b.webp" master_src="https://qph.ec.quoracdn.net/main-qimg-8274f4fa4b96e458a73c051196a11675" master_w="749" master_h="540">
                     </div><p class="qtext_para"><b>FOR GMAIL AND GOOGLE APPS ACCOUNTS</b></p><p class="qtext_para">Go to Googles password assistance page at <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.google.com/accounts/recovery" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 48717589);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="google.com">google.com/accounts/recovery</a></span> and choose the <i>I dont know my password</i> option. Enter the email address that you are trying to verify  it could be an @gmail address or a Google Apps address  and choose Continue.                     <span class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">Yeah, its pretty easy thing nowaday. To check whether an email address is valid or not, you can <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.accuwebhosting.com/blog/top-10-bulk-email-list-verification-validation-services-compared/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 46844957);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="accuwebhosting.com"><b><i>use validation/verification services</i></b></a></span>. Once email addresses are verified, you can download the cleaned list on your local computer.</p><p class="qtext_para">To automate an email validation process, you can use the email validation APIs provided by these providers. This API can be integrated with your web form or site, and lets you verify email addresses as user insert it. An email address you insert to validate will be verified through following check points.</p>
                     <ol>
                      <li><b>Email address syntax check</b>: This checkpoint removes any improperly formatted email addresses.</li>
                      <li><b>Domain/MX records check</b>: This process will check the DNS entries of an email address.</li>
                      <li><b>Role accounts detection</b> =&gt; This process detects and flags the role account email addresses.</li>
                      <li><b>Disposable email address (DEA) detection</b> =&gt; This check point will identify whether an email address is a disposable email address.</li>
                      <li><b>Abuse detection</b> =&gt; It detects frequent complainers who are known for marking mails as spam.</li>
                      <li><b>Spamtraps/honeypots</b> =&gt; At this stage, The provider should be able to identify Spamtraps.</li>
                      <li><b>Checking DNSBLs and URI DNSBLs for blacklisting</b> =&gt;In this process, email address or IP address is checked against the known DNSBLs and URI DNSBLs to trap spam networks.</li>
                      <li><b>Final verification </b>=&gt; This phase performs deep level SMTP verification on each email address. Each email address will be verified for mailbox existence without sending actual emails on that address.</li>
                     </ol></span>

                    <span class="rendered_qtext">Not really.&nbsp; Each domain is responsible for handling email sent to it. If the joe.smith email address is configured to forward to a <span class="qlink_container"><a href="/topic/Gmail">Gmail</a></span> account, does it exist? Or what if the domain is configured so that it has a "default" mail account that all mail is routed to if there's not another account defined? Or if the mail server accepts an email to that address, but immediately deletes it?<br><br>If you want to know whether an email will bounce, the only way you'll really know is if you try sending it, and see what happens.</span>
