            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>pdf-extract</h1> 
         <p>A tool and library that can extract various areas of text from a PDF, especially a scholarly article PDF. It performs structural analysis to determine column bounds, headers, footers, sections, titles and so on. It can analyse and categorise sections into reference and non-reference sections and can split reference sections into individual references.</p> 
         <p>The latest version is <em>0.1.1</em>. Earlier versions are far less reliable.</p> 
         <p>pdf-extract requires Ruby 1.9.1 or above.</p> 
         <h2><a id="user-content-quick-start" class="anchor" href="#quick-start" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>Quick start</h2> 
         <p>Install the latest version with:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ gem install pdf-extract
</code></pre> 
         <h2><a id="user-content-quick-examples" class="anchor" href="#quick-examples" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>Quick examples</h2> 
         <p>Extract references from a PDF:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract --references myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>Extract references and a title from a PDF:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract --references --titles myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>Mark the locations of headers, footers and columns in a new PDF:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract mark --columns --headers --footers myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>Extract regions of text from a PDF, preserving line information (offsets from region origin):</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract --regions myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>Extract regions of text from a PDF without line information (prettier and easier to read):</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract --regions --no-lines myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>Resolve references to DOIs and output related metadata as BibTeX:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract-bib --resolved_references myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <h2><a id="user-content-problems" class="anchor" href="#problems" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>Problems</h2> 
         <h3><a id="user-content-pdf-extract-mistakes-normal-text-for-references-when-attempting-to-extract-references" class="anchor" href="#pdf-extract-mistakes-normal-text-for-references-when-attempting-to-extract-references" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>pdf-extract mistakes normal text for references when attempting to extract references.</h3> 
         <p>pdf-extract attempts to identify reference sections by comparing section features to an idealised model of a reference section. Sometimes this can go wrong. If pdf-extract is producing reference output that clearly includes something that is not a reference, try reducing the <code>reference_flex</code> slightly:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract --references --set reference_flex:0.18 myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>The default for <code>reference_flex</code> is 0.2. Make small decrements.</p> 
         <h3><a id="user-content-pdf-extract-extracts-no-references" class="anchor" href="#pdf-extract-extracts-no-references" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>pdf-extract extracts no references.</h3> 
         <p>As above, but try to increase the reference_flex a bit a time:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract extract --references --set reference_flex:0.25 myfile.pdf
</code></pre> 
         <p>Keep trying with small increments to reference_flex. Note that a reference_flex of 1 means pdf-extract will identify <em>all</em> sections as reference sections.</p> 
         <h3><a id="user-content-pdf-extract-is-still-producing-weird-output-after-fiddling-with-reference_flex" class="anchor" href="#pdf-extract-is-still-producing-weird-output-after-fiddling-with-reference_flex" aria-hidden="true">
           <svg aria-hidden="true" class="octicon octicon-link" height="16" version="1.1" viewbox="0 0 16 16" width="16">
            <path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path>
           </svg></a>pdf-extract is still producing weird output after fiddling with reference_flex.</h3> 
         <p>Have a look at pdf-extract's settings:</p> 
         <pre><code>$ pdf-extract settings
</code></pre> 
         <p>This command will produce a list of settings along with descriptions of what they affect. They can be set by passing a <code>--set key:value</code> argument to pdf-extract.</p>
