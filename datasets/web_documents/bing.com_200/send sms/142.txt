         <ul> 
          <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Recipient's Phone Number:&nbsp; Captures the recipient's cellular telephone number (10 digit)</font> </li>
          <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Recipient's Carrier:&nbsp; Captures the recipient's carrier.</font> </li>
          <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Sender's email address:&nbsp; Captures the sender's email address.</font> </li>
          <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Sender's email server:&nbsp; Captures the name of the sender's email server&nbsp;</font> </li>
          <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Message Subject Line:&nbsp; Captures the message's title or subject</font> </li>
          <li><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Message Body:&nbsp; Captures the sender's message content.</font></li>
         </ul><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"> <p>The application is simple but could easily be improved by validating each of the required fields through the use of regular expressions or by at least validating that the text associated with each of the text boxes is not an empty string.&nbsp; To maintain the simplicity of the project, little in the way of error handling has been included.</p> <p>The following figure (Figure 1) shows a properly configured collection of input fields in use:</p> <p><img alt="" hspace="0" src="Images/SendTxtMsg1.gif" align="baseline" border="0"></p> </font></font>

        <p class="MsoNormal" style="MARGIN: 0in 0in 0pt; LINE-HEIGHT: normal; mso-layout-grid-align: none"><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif"></font><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">At this point, the application should be complete you may wish to build the solution and test it.&nbsp; Even though this example was intended to be simple, the overall concept may be used within an application to do some seemingly complex jobs.&nbsp; For example, if you were tasked with writing an application that monitored some sort of trend information such as a daily stock price, and were to alert a group of end users whenever the stock price exceeded some predetermined, agreed upon value, you could do something such as looping through a collection of users subscribing to the stock price monitoring service and direct a text message to each of these users indicating that the watched stock had surpassed the threshold value.&nbsp; </font></p> 
        <p><font face="Verdana, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif">Also please note that, whilst it does cost you a dime to send a message to a cell phone in this manner, it may well cost the recipient something to receive it.&nbsp; Bearing that in mind, as you test your version of the code, be mindful of an expenses you may be generating for yourself (if, for example, you are sending messages to yourself) or another person.</font></p> 
        <p><span style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; COLOR: #ff6600; FONT-FAMILY: Verdana">NOTE</span><span style="FONT-SIZE: 10pt; COLOR: #ff6600; FONT-FAMILY: Verdana" nd="11">: THIS ARTICLE IS CONVERTED FROM VB.NET TO&nbsp;C#&nbsp;USING A CONVERSION TOOL.
