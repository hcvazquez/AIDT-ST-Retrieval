           <p><em>The internet has transformed the way many of us listen to music. With singles such as Gnarls Barkley's Crazy topping the charts on their digital sales alone, and new sites emerging all the time, here are some of the best places to listen to and download music online.</em></p>

           </div>

           <p style="font-style: italic;">We've put a guide together to listening to music online and downloading legally including details on how much it is going to cost you.</p>
           <p><span style="font-weight: bold;"></span></p>
           <p><span style="font-weight: bold;">iTunes</span></p>
           <p>The deal: A la carte download service with a 6m plus catalogue size. You will need to download or update your version of iTunes. It includes track reviews and genius functionality which will recommend users music.</p>
           <p>The cost: There is no monthly cost. The average price of a single download is 79p and albums start at 7.99.</p>
           <p style="font-weight: bold;">Napster</p>
           <p>The deal: The website offers two packages, Napster Unlimited which is a paid for subscription based streaming service and Napster 'on the go', which is a subscription based music renting service. The website has an estimated catalogue size of 8m plus tracks. It also has a recommendation service to advice you on similar music to bands you are already searching for. The 'on the go' package gives you instant access to your music playlists from anywhere in the world.</p>
           <p>The cost: The price per month depends on the package. Napster Unlimited costs 5 per month or 50 a year, its unlimited service plus mobile costs 10 a month or 100 for the year.&nbsp; Downloads cost from 49p for a single and 4.99 for an album - both packages include unlimited streaming. <br></p>
           <span style="font-weight: bold;"></span>
           <div class="clear"> 
           </div>
           <div class="thinCenter"> 
            <img src="data:image/gif;base64,R0lGODlhAQABAIAAAAAAAP///yH5BAEAAAAALAAAAAABAAEAAAIBRAA7" data-src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2011/06/16/article-1602197-05324C4A0000044D-366_468x286.jpg" height="286" width="468" alt="Thom Yorke, lead singer of the British band Radiohead" class="blkBorder"> 
            <noscript> 
             <img src="http://i.dailymail.co.uk/i/pix/2011/06/16/article-1602197-05324C4A0000044D-366_468x286.jpg" height="286" width="468" alt="Thom Yorke, lead singer of the British band Radiohead" class="blkBorder"> 
            </noscript> 
            <p class="imageCaption">Little by little: Thom Yorke, lead singer of the British band Radiohead</p>
           </div> 
           <p> </p>
           <p style="font-weight: bold;">Spotify</p>
           <p>The deal: Ad-supported subscription based music streaming site with access to over 6m tracks. It recently introduced a download service. <br></p>
           <p>The cost: The site is free to access but earlier this year the free listening time was cut from 20 hours to 10 hours. Tracks can only be listened to a maximum of five times before they can no longer be played. The application, which is downloaded to your computer desktop, also offers subscription packages. <br></p>
           <p>It costs 4.99 a month to subscribe to Spotify's unlimited service (with unlimited music listening and no adverts), or 9.99 a month to subscribe to its premium package, which includes Spotify on your mobile and being able to play it without being connected to the internet.<br></p>
           <p><span style="font-weight: bold;">Play.com</span><br><br>The deal: An online download website with no additional software to download.<br><br>The cost: There is no monthly cost. Single track downloads cost 65p and album's cost 8.95<br></p>

            <p class="imageCaption">The fear: Lily Allen performs at music festival T in the Park in 2009<br></p>

           <p> </p>
           <p style="font-weight: bold;">HMV</p> 
           <p>The deal: Online download service and paid for streaming. They have a catalogue of 4m plus tracks.</p> 
           <p>The cost: Downloads cost 0.99 for tracks and 8.99 for albums, there's no additional monthly cost. It is 5.99 per month for unlimited monthly streaming.</p> 
           <p style="font-weight: bold;">7digital</p> 
           <p>The deal: Online download service. They have a catalogue of 4m plus tracks.</p> 
           <p>The cost: No monthly cost. Single track downloads cost 79p and album's cost 5.</p>

            <p>The deal: Subscription based download service with 6m plus tracks available.</p> 
            <p>The cost: Single track and album downloads with subscription. The cost depends on the package. Package 1 costs 9.99 per month for 24 downloads. Package 2 costs 13.99 per month for 35 downloads. Package 3 costs 17.99 per month for 50 downloads.</p>

            <p>The deal: Primarily online streaming service with additional downloads. A catalogue of 3.5m tracks available.</p> 
            <p>The cost: There is no monthly cost to access the streaming service. Single tracks cost 97p to download and albums cost 6.85.</p>
