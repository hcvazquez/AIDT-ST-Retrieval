        <h1>How do I use the "audience filters" or "maturity levels" setting on my library's OverDrive website?</h1>
        <a class="print-icon icon-print" data-reveal-id="printModal" href="#"><span>Print </span></a> 
       </div> 
      </div>
     </div> 
     <div class="row"> 
      <!-- Left sidebar -->

           <div class="row preview-selection"> 
            <h5 data-mc-conditions="Default.External">Choose the image below that looks like the titles in your library's collection.</h5> 
            <div class="large-6 columns" data-mc-conditions="Default.English-Only">
             <a href="#nextgencontent" title="Hit Enter if you're using an OverDrive website whose first skip link is Site Navigation" class="nextgen-style selected"></a> 
            </div> 
            <div class="large-6 columns" data-mc-conditions="Default.English-Only">
             <a href="#thundercontent" title="Hit Enter if you're using a new OverDrive website whose first skip link is skip to main content" class="thunder-style selected"></a> 
            </div> 
            <div class="large-12 columns thunder" id="thundercontent"> 
             <p>You can use the audience filters to limit the type(s) of content you'll see while browsing and searching your library's OverDrive website. </p> 
             <p> The audience filters you can choose from are:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li value="1"><strong>Juvenile:</strong> Fiction and nonfiction titles for children.</li> 
              <li value="2"><strong>Young adult:</strong> Fiction and nonfiction titles for young adults.</li> 
              <li value="3"><strong>General adult:</strong> General fiction and nonfiction.</li> 
              <li value="4"><strong>Mature adult:</strong> Fiction and nonfiction for readers over the age of 18 (for example, erotic literature).</li> 
             </ul> 
             <h2>How to choose audience filters for your account</h2> 
             <ol> 
              <li value="1">On your library's OverDrive website, click or tap <img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/articles/Images/thunder_loans_icon_35x35.png" alt="books icon" class="inline" style="width: 35;height: 35;">, then select <strong>Settings</strong>.<br>You'll need to be <a href="https://overdrive.desk.com/customer/portal/articles/2166600">signed into the site</a> to get to this page.</li> 
              <li value="2">Under "Audience filters," select the type(s) of content you'd like to see while browsing and searching your library's OverDrive website. Your settings are saved automatically.<br><img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/articles/Images/thunder_settings page_audience filter.jpg" class="borders" alt="audience filters setting"><br>If you choose all four audience types, the setting will automatically revert to <strong>All audiences</strong>.</li> 
             </ol> 
            </div> 
            <div class="large-12 columns nextgen" id="nextgencontent"> 
             <p>Your library may allow you to exclude content categorized under certain "maturity levels" while browsing and searching your library's OverDrive website. </p> 
             <p>The maturity levels you can choose from are:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li value="1"><strong>Juvenile</strong>: Fiction and nonfiction titles for children.</li> 
              <li value="2"><strong>Young Adult</strong>: Fiction and nonfiction titles for young adults.</li> 
              <li value="3"><strong>General Adult</strong>: General fiction and nonfiction.</li> 
              <li value="4"><strong>Mature Adult</strong>: Fiction and nonfiction for readers over the age of 18 (for example, erotic literature). </li> 
             </ul> 
             <h2>How to choose your maturity level(s)</h2> 
             <ol> 
              <li value="1">Log into your <strong>Account</strong> on your library's OverDrive website.</li> 
              <li value="2">Select your <strong>Settings</strong> page. </li> 
              <li value="3">Use the "Maturity level(s)" setting (if available) to choose the range of content that you would like to see. <br><img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/articles/Images/maturity levels 2.png" alt="Maturity levels settings" class="borders"><br>For example, if you never wanted to see mature adult content, you would select <strong>Juvenile</strong> to <strong>General Adult</strong>. If you never wanted to see juvenile or young adult content, you would select <strong>General Adult</strong> to <strong>Mature Adult</strong>. If you want to see everything, keep <strong>Juvenile</strong> to <strong>Mature Adult</strong> selected.</li> 
              <li value="4">Select <strong>Save</strong> to save your maturity settings before you leave the page.</li> 
             </ol> 
             <p>Your library may also allow you to <a href="https://overdrive.desk.com/customer/portal/articles/1647469">hide adult cover images</a> while you're browsing or searching your library's digital collection.</p> 
             <p><em><strong>Note</strong>: Content in your library's digital collection will only be filtered based on your "Maturity level(s)" setting when you're signed into your library account.</em> </p>

       <h1>How do I use the "audience filters" or "maturity levels" setting on my library's OverDrive website?</h1>
       <a class="printMe"><img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/artifacts/img/print_icon.png" width="50" alt="print it"></a>

       <div class="large-12 columns thunder" id="thundercontent"> 
        <p>You can use the audience filters to limit the type(s) of content you'll see while browsing and searching your library's OverDrive website. </p> 
        <p> The audience filters you can choose from are:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li value="1"><strong>Juvenile:</strong> Fiction and nonfiction titles for children.</li> 
         <li value="2"><strong>Young adult:</strong> Fiction and nonfiction titles for young adults.</li> 
         <li value="3"><strong>General adult:</strong> General fiction and nonfiction.</li> 
         <li value="4"><strong>Mature adult:</strong> Fiction and nonfiction for readers over the age of 18 (for example, erotic literature).</li> 
        </ul> 
        <h2>How to choose audience filters for your account</h2> 
        <ol> 
         <li value="1">On your library's OverDrive website, click or tap <img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/articles/Images/thunder_loans_icon_35x35.png" alt="books icon" class="inline" style="width: 35;height: 35;">, then select <strong>Settings</strong>.<br>You'll need to be <a href="https://overdrive.desk.com/customer/portal/articles/2166600">signed into the site</a> to get to this page.</li> 
         <li value="2">Under "Audience filters," select the type(s) of content you'd like to see while browsing and searching your library's OverDrive website. Your settings are saved automatically.<br><img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/articles/Images/thunder_settings page_audience filter.jpg" class="borders" alt="audience filters setting"><br>If you choose all four audience types, the setting will automatically revert to <strong>All audiences</strong>.</li> 
        </ol> 
       </div> 
       <div class="large-12 columns nextgen" id="nextgencontent"> 
        <p>Your library may allow you to exclude content categorized under certain "maturity levels" while browsing and searching your library's OverDrive website. </p> 
        <p>The maturity levels you can choose from are:</p> 
        <ul> 
         <li value="1"><strong>Juvenile</strong>: Fiction and nonfiction titles for children.</li> 
         <li value="2"><strong>Young Adult</strong>: Fiction and nonfiction titles for young adults.</li> 
         <li value="3"><strong>General Adult</strong>: General fiction and nonfiction.</li> 
         <li value="4"><strong>Mature Adult</strong>: Fiction and nonfiction for readers over the age of 18 (for example, erotic literature). </li> 
        </ul> 
        <h2>How to choose your maturity level(s)</h2> 
        <ol> 
         <li value="1">Log into your <strong>Account</strong> on your library's OverDrive website.</li> 
         <li value="2">Select your <strong>Settings</strong> page. </li> 
         <li value="3">Use the "Maturity level(s)" setting (if available) to choose the range of content that you would like to see. <br><img src="https://odhelp.blob.core.windows.net/content/articles/Images/maturity levels 2.png" alt="Maturity levels settings" class="borders"><br>For example, if you never wanted to see mature adult content, you would select <strong>Juvenile</strong> to <strong>General Adult</strong>. If you never wanted to see juvenile or young adult content, you would select <strong>General Adult</strong> to <strong>Mature Adult</strong>. If you want to see everything, keep <strong>Juvenile</strong> to <strong>Mature Adult</strong> selected.</li> 
         <li value="4">Select <strong>Save</strong> to save your maturity settings before you leave the page.</li> 
        </ol> 
        <p>Your library may also allow you to <a href="https://overdrive.desk.com/customer/portal/articles/1647469">hide adult cover images</a> while you're browsing or searching your library's digital collection.</p> 
        <p><em><strong>Note</strong>: Content in your library's digital collection will only be filtered based on your "Maturity level(s)" setting when you're signed into your library account.</em> </p>
