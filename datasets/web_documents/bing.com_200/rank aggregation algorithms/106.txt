             <h3 xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml" id="sites-page-title-header" style="" align="left"> <span id="sites-page-title" dir="ltr" tabindex="-1" style="outline: none">Outlier Detection</span> </h3> 
             <div id="sites-canvas-main" class="sites-canvas-main"> 
              <div id="sites-canvas-main-content"> 
               <div xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1999/xhtml" class="sites-layout-name-right-sidebar-hf sites-layout-vbox">
                <div class="sites-layout-tile sites-tile-name-header">
                 <div dir="ltr">

                    <div dir="ltr">
                     This page shows an example on outlier detection with the LOF (Local Outlier Factor) algorithm.
                     <br>
                     <h3><a name="TOC-The-LOF-algorithm"></a>The LOF algorithm</h3>LOF (Local Outlier Factor) is an algorithm for identifying density-based local outliers [Breunig et al., 2000]. With LOF, the local density of a point is compared with that of its neighbors. If the former is signi.cantly lower than the latter (with an LOF value greater than one), the point is in a sparser region than its neighbors, which suggests it be an outlier.
                     <br>
                     <br>Function lofactor(data, k) in packages DMwR and dprep calculates local outlier factors using the LOF algorithm, where k is the number of neighbors used in the calculation of the local outlier factors.
                     <br>
                     <h3><a name="TOC-Calculate-Outlier-Scores"></a>Calculate Outlier Scores</h3>
                     <div class="sites-codeblock sites-codesnippet-block">
                      <code>&gt; library(DMwR)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; # remove "Species", which is a categorical column</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; iris2 &lt;- iris[,1:4]</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; outlier.scores &lt;- lofactor(iris2, k=5)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; plot(density(outlier.scores))</code>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div style="display:block;text-align:left">
                      <a href="http://www.rdatamining.com/examples/outlier-detection/density-plot.jpg?attredirects=0" imageanchor="1"><img alt="A density plot of outlier scores" border="0" src="http://www.rdatamining.com/_/rsrc/1421497372484/examples/outlier-detection/density-plot.jpg"></a>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div class="sites-codeblock sites-codesnippet-block">
                      <code>&gt; # pick top 5 as outliers</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; outliers &lt;- order(outlier.scores, decreasing=T)[1:5]</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; # who are outliers</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; print(outliers)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>[1] 42 107 23 110 63</code>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <h3><a name="TOC-Visualize-Outliers-with-Plots"></a>Visualize Outliers with Plots</h3>Next, we show outliers with a biplot of the first two principal components.
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div class="sites-codeblock sites-codesnippet-block">
                      <code>&gt; n &lt;- nrow(iris2)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; labels &lt;- 1:n</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; labels[-outliers] &lt;- "."</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; biplot(prcomp(iris2), cex=.8, xlabs=labels)</code>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div style="display:block;text-align:left">
                      <a href="http://www.rdatamining.com/examples/outlier-detection/biplot.jpg?attredirects=0" imageanchor="1"><img alt="Visualization of outliers in a biplot of the 1st two principal components" border="0" src="http://www.rdatamining.com/_/rsrc/1421497384778/examples/outlier-detection/biplot.jpg"></a>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>We can also show outliers with a pairs plot as below, where outliers are labeled with "+" in red.
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div class="sites-codeblock sites-codesnippet-block">
                      <code>&gt; pch &lt;- rep(".", n)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; pch[outliers] &lt;- "+"</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; col &lt;- rep("black", n)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; col[outliers] &lt;- "red"</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; pairs(iris2, pch=pch, col=col)</code>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div style="display:block;text-align:left">
                      <a href="http://www.rdatamining.com/examples/outlier-detection/pairs-plot.jpg?attredirects=0" imageanchor="1"><img alt="Visualization of outliers with a matrix of scatter plots" border="0" src="http://www.rdatamining.com/_/rsrc/1421497358880/examples/outlier-detection/pairs-plot.jpg"></a>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <h3><a name="TOC-Parallel-Computation-of-LOF-Scores"></a>Parallel Computation of LOF Scores</h3>Package Rlof provides function lof(), a parallel implementation of the LOF algorithm. Its usage is similar to the above lofactor(), but lof() has two additional features&nbsp;of supporting multiple values of k and several choices of distance metrics. Below is an example of lof(). 
                     <br>
                     <br>
                     <div class="sites-codeblock sites-codesnippet-block">
                      <code>&gt; library(Rlof)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; outlier.scores &lt;- lof(iris2, k=5)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; # try with different number of neighbors (k = 5,6,7,8,9 and 10)</code>
                      <br>
                      <code>&gt; outlier.scores &lt;- lof(iris2, k=c(5:10))</code>
                     </div>
                     <br>
                     <br>
