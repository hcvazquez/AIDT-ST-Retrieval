                       Badrish
                       <br>
                       <br> Your Date field must be a 
                       <b>Text Field</b> (not Static Text) for this to work.
                       <br> In case you have a static field, just do this:
                       <br> Right click on the date field and select 
                       <b>Transform in Text Field</b> (F3).
                       <br> This will convert your static text element into a text field element.
                       <br>
                       <br> Now, right click your date text field.
                       <br> Select 
                       <b>properties</b> and click on the 
                       <b>Text Field</b> tab.
                       <br> In the 
                       <b>Text Field Expression Class</b> select 
                       <b>java.util.Date</b>.
                       <br> I am assuming that you are passing a date parameter as you have mentioned.
                       <br> In the 
                       <b>Evaluation Time</b>, select 
                       <b>Now</b>.
                       <br> In the 
                       <b>Pattern</b> type in "
                       <i>MM/dd/yyyy</i>" or whatever pattern you need.
                       <br> Lastly, in the 
                       <b>text field expression</b> indicate the parameter that you are using like $P{importantDate}.
                       <br>
                       <br> hth!

                       If your input String format is "YYYY-MM-DD", Let's assume $P{date} has the date in String.
                       <br>
                       <br>
                       <br>
                       <table cellpadding="0" cellspacing="0" align="center" width="525" bgcolor="#cccccc">
                        <tbody>
                         <tr>
                          <td>(new GregorianCalendar(Integer.parseInt($P{date}.substring(0,4)),Integer.parseInt($P{date}.substring(5,7))-1,Integer.parseInt($P{date}.substring(8,10)))).getTime()</td>
                         </tr>
                        </tbody>
                       </table>
                       <br>
                       <br> This will convert your String to java.util.Date Object. Then, you can specify Pattern such as dd/MM/yyy
                       <br>
                       <br> Takashi
