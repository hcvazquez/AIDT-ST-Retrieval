                  <div class="field-item even">
                   <p style="margin: 0.75em 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-variant-numeric: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 20px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">Eduardo Rosa-Molinar, Ph.D. is the Director of the Microscopy and Analytical Imaging Resource Core Laboratory (<a href="http://mai.ku.edu/eduardo-rosa-molinar-phd" target="_blank">http://mai.ku.edu/eduardo-rosa-molinar-phd</a>), Professor of Pharmacology and Toxicology and Neuroscience Graduate Program (<a href="http://pharmtox.ku.edu/all-faculty" target="_blank">http://pharmtox.ku.edu/all-faculty</a>; <a href="http://neuroscience.ku.edu/faculty-members" target="_blank">http://neuroscience.ku.edu/faculty-members</a>), and Professor of Anatomy and Cell Biology (<a href="http://www.kumc.edu/school-of-medicine/anatomy-and-cell-biology/secondary-appointment-faculty.html" target="_blank">http://www.kumc.edu/school-of-medicine/anatomy-and-cell-biology/secondary-appointment-faculty.html</a>) at the University of Kansas and the University of Kansas Medical Center respectively. &nbsp;He is also a Whitman Scientist at the Whitman Center, Marine Biological Laboratory (<a href="http://www.mbl.edu/research/mbl-scientists/" target="_blank">http://www.mbl.edu/research/mbl-scientists/</a>) and Course Director of the Immunohistochemistry and Microscopy, a special topic course taught at the Marine Biological Laboratory, Woods Hole, MA (<a href="http://www.mbl.edu/education/special-topics-courses/immunohistochemistry-and-microscopy-ihcm/" target="_blank">http://www.mbl.edu/education/special-topics-courses/immunohistochemistry-and-microscopy-ihcm/</a>).&nbsp; Until June 2015, he was a tenured Professor of Integrative Anatomy and Neurobiology, University of Puerto Rico-Rio Piedras, San Juan Puerto Rico.</p> 
                   <p style="margin: 0.75em 0px 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-variant-numeric: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; line-height: 20px; background-color: rgb(255, 255, 255);">His research focuses on neural microcircuits", a specific pattern of interconnections between neurons and synapses within a specific region of the central nervous system (<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; font-size: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit;">i.e.,</em>&nbsp;spinal cord); he images and studies the three-dimensional (3-D) nano-scale architecture of mixed synapses, a poorly studied synapse that combines the features of both chemical and electrical synapses (i<em style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; border: 0px; vertical-align: baseline; font-variant: inherit; font-weight: inherit; font-stretch: inherit; font-size: inherit; line-height: inherit; font-family: inherit;">.e.,</em>&nbsp;gap junction).&nbsp; To this end, he develops quantitative immuno-correlative photon- and electron-based imaging technologies required to image and analyze in 3-D the nano-scale membrane organization of mixed synapses pre-and post-synaptic membrane proteins.&nbsp; The way in which the 3-D the nano-scale membrane organization of mixed synapses pre-and post-synaptic membrane proteins present therein, structural and topographical information is critical to guide structure-based drug design and discovery.</p>
