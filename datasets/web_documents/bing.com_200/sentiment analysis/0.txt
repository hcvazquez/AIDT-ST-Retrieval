            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>Sentiment analysis doensn't keep up with the hyped promises.</p> 
              <p>See e.g.</p> 
              <p><strong>The Sad State of Sentiment Analysis</strong><br> December 26, 2013 by Angela Hausman<br> <a href="http://www.hausmanmarketingletter.com/sad-state-sentiment-analysis/">http://www.hausmanmarketingletter.com/sad-state-sentiment-analysis/</a></p> 
              <blockquote> 
               <p>Recent experiments suggest sentiment analysis data is LESS accurate than a coin toss (accuracy 50%). That’s really scary if your brand makes strategic decisions based on sentiment analysis.</p> 
              </blockquote> 
              <p>...</p> 
              <blockquote> 
               <p>While the tools accurately predicted between 60 and 80% of utterances, when neutral utterances were removed (80% of the utterances) the accuracy dropped alarmingly.</p> 
              </blockquote> 
              <p>In other words, everybody is cheating on their benchmarks, and overfitting (e.g. tweets have tons of duplicates and near duplicates - retweets - if you include these, you are overestimating the real performance)</p>
