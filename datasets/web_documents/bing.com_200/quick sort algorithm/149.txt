          <h4>Transcript of Bubble Sort, Quick Sort, Merge Sort, &amp; Shell Sort</h4>
          <div class="prezi-text-content clamped">
           Bubble Sort
           <br>Bubble sort is a simple sorting algorithm that works by repeatedly stepping through the list to be sorted, comparing each pair of adjacent items and swapping them if they are in the wrong order. 
           <br>The first and second elements are compared and swapped if out of order. Then the second and third elements are compared and swapped if out of order. This sorting process continues until the last two elements of the array are compared and swapped if out of order.
           <br>Bubble sort is never used for more then 5000.
           <br>Shell Sort
           <br>Shellsort, also known as Shell sort or Shell's method, is an in-place comparison sort.
           <br>It generalizes an exchanging sort, such as insertion or bubble sort, by starting the comparison and exchange of elements with elements that are far apart before finishing with neighboring elements. 
           <br>Shell sort breaks down large arrays into a series of small sections which it then treats as though they were "Insertion Sort". 
           <br>Starting with far apart elements can move some out-of-place elements into position faster than a simple nearest neighbor exchange.
           <br>Merge Sort
           <br>Merge sort works in a very simple way to sort numbers. The list of numbers is divided into smaller sublists containing two numbers each. 
           <br>The two numbers are put into order from smaller to largest. The first number in the sublist is compared with the first number in the other sublist. 
           <br>The numbers are put into order. This creates more sublists. 
           <br>These sublists are compares again until the while list is sorted. 
           <br>Merge sort is better at sorting lists because it requires fewer steps.
           <br>Quick Sort
           <br>Quick sort works by taking a random number from the array and selecting it as the "pivot". It then sorts the list so that the numbers larger then the pivot are on the right side and the numbers smaller then the pivot are on the left side.
           <br>1. The program goes through the list from the left side and finds a number that is greater than or equal to the pivot.
           <br>2. The program goes through the list from the right side and finds a number that is less than or equal to the pivot.
           <br>3. The program switches the two numbers.
           <br>Quick sort is very complex. It requires a lot of coding while the bubble sort is fairly easy. Although quick sort is more efficient even with larger lists.
