      <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Linear_Algebra">Linear Algebra</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Programming/Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Linear Algebra">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2> 
      <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Vectors">Vectors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Programming/Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Vectors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_inner_product">The inner product</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Programming/Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: The inner product">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>The inner product is also called the <i>dot product</i> or the <i>scalar product</i>. It is the sum of the item-by-item product.</p> 
      <div class="mw-highlight mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr"> 
       <pre>
<span class="o">&gt;</span> u <span class="o">&lt;-</span> <span class="kp">rep</span><span class="p">(</span><span class="m">3</span><span class="p">,</span><span class="m">3</span><span class="p">)</span>
<span class="o">&gt;</span> v <span class="o">&lt;-</span> <span class="m">1</span><span class="o">:</span><span class="m">3</span>
<span class="o">&gt;</span> u<span class="o">%*%</span>v <span class="c1"># the inner product</span>
     <span class="p">[,</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="p">]</span>
<span class="p">[</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="p">,]</span>   <span class="m">18</span>
</pre>
      </div> 
      <h4><span class="mw-headline" id="The_outer_product">The outer product</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Programming/Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The outer product">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4> 
      <p>The outer product is also called the <i>cross product</i> or the <i>vector product</i>. It is a matrix resulting from the product of the elements of the two vectors.</p> 
      <div class="mw-highlight mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr"> 
       <pre>
<span class="o">&gt;</span> v <span class="o">&lt;-</span> <span class="kp">rep</span><span class="p">(</span><span class="m">3</span><span class="p">,</span><span class="m">3</span><span class="p">)</span>
<span class="o">&gt;</span> u <span class="o">&lt;-</span> <span class="m">1</span><span class="o">:</span><span class="m">3</span>
<span class="o">&gt;</span> u<span class="o">%o%</span>v <span class="c1"># The outer product</span>
     <span class="p">[,</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="p">]</span> <span class="p">[,</span><span class="m">2</span><span class="p">]</span> <span class="p">[,</span><span class="m">3</span><span class="p">]</span>

      <p>If you want to create a new matrix, one way is to use the <tt>matrix()</tt> function.       <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_factorial_function">The factorial function</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=R_Programming/Mathematics&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: The factorial function">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3> 
      <p><tt>factorial</tt> returns the factorial of an integer. This can also be computed using the <tt>prod()</tt> (product) applied to the vector of integers between 1 and the number of interest.</p> 
      <div class="mw-highlight mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr"> 
       <pre>

<span class="o">&gt;</span> <span class="kp">prod</span><span class="p">(</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="o">:</span><span class="m">3</span><span class="p">)</span>
<span class="p">[</span><span class="m">1</span><span class="p">]</span> <span class="m">6</span>
</pre>
      </div> 
      <p>Note that by convention <span class="mwe-math-element"><span class="mwe-math-mathml-inline mwe-math-mathml-a11y" style="display: none;">
         <math xmlns="http://www.w3.org/1998/Math/MathML"> 
          <semantics> 
           <mrow class="MJX-TeXAtom-ORD"> 
            <mstyle displaystyle="true" scriptlevel="0"> 
             <mn>
              0
             </mn> 
             <mo>
              !
             </mo> 
             <mo>
              =
             </mo> 
             <mn>
              1
             </mn> 
            </mstyle> 
           </mrow> 
           <annotation encoding="application/x-tex">
            {\displaystyle 0!=1}
           </annotation> 
          </semantics>
