           <h3 name="b6f7" id="b6f7" class="graf graf--h3 graf-after--p">Criteria to choose your javascript charting&nbsp;library</h3>
           <p name="1f8c" id="1f8c" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h3">It is important to understand what your project needs are and how they will evolve. This way you will save precious time by working with the right libraries from the start.</p>
           <p name="7b71" id="7b71" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">There are many elements to consider that can be equally important:</p>
           <ul class="postList">
            <li name="673a" id="673a" class="graf graf--li graf-after--p"><strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">License</strong> used by the libraries</li>
            <li name="35cf" id="35cf" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li">The library <strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">enrichment and maintenance over time</strong>. This can be assessed through the libraries’ Github repository popularity and activity.</li>
            <li name="38e5" id="38e5" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li">The <strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">chart types provided</strong> (2D, 3D, map, scientific, financial, and statistical charting)</li>
            <li name="a57d" id="a57d" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li">The <strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">customisation options</strong> on your charts (titles, legends, zoom, tooltip, responsiveness)</li>
            <li name="4de5" id="4de5" class="graf graf--li graf-after--li"><strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong">Charts design </strong>is also important.<strong class="markup--strong markup--li-strong"> </strong>But I believe it is mostly a matter personal taste. Therefore it will not be extensively reviewed. You can still find an example of how the bar charts look a the end of the article.</li>
           </ul>
           <p name="9aeb" id="9aeb" class="graf graf--p graf-after--li">Other criteria that are not (or partially) reviewed in this article: browsers compatibility and animation options.</p>
           <p name="90ac" id="90ac" class="graf graf--p graf-after--p">Once you know what matters for your project, the next parts of the article will help you find the right library.</p>

           <p name="60f9" id="60f9" class="graf graf--p graf-after--h4 graf--trailing">Some of you might be tempted to think: the more options and provided graphs the better. Then why not choose Plotly for all the projects since it offers the largest diversity of charts? This is not the right approach.<strong class="markup--strong markup--p-strong"> If your needs are fulfilled by a simple lightweight library like Chartist, then go for it</strong>. Keep it simple. It will save you time by not diving into unnecessary options. Moreover your application would load faster with lighter dependencies. Once again it all depends on your project needs and how you think they will evolve.</p>
