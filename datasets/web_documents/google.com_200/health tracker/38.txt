        <p>Recently I have been given the opportunity to review a HELO smartband, a comprehensive health monitor which you wear on your wrist. HELO stands for Health and Lifestyle Oracle. Unlike other wrist band monitors, what interested me is that this band not only monitors steps and heart rate but claims it can also measure your blood pressure, ECG, respiration and even your energy levels and mood! All the data syncs with an app on your smartphone (Android or iOS) and data can be shared with a loved one (imagine using it to monitor your elderly parents) or even your physician. It even comes with a Panic button mode so that you can summon help.</p> 
        <p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6811" src="http://palmdoc.net/images/helo5-300x101.jpg" alt="helo5" width="300" height="101" srcset="https://palmdoc.net/images/helo5-300x101.jpg 300w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo5.jpg 672w" sizes="(max-width: 300px) 100vw, 300px"></p> 
        <p>Here’s a short video on the features:<br> <iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/xrmGb4-JYak" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen></iframe></p> 
        <p>Sounds too good to be true for just a wrist band? Read on.</p> 
        <p><strong>Unboxing</strong><br> The smartband comes in an unassuming black square packaging.<br> <img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6805" src="http://palmdoc.net/images/helo2-289x300.jpg" alt="helo2" width="256" height="300"></p> 
        <p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6804" src="http://palmdoc.net/images/helo1-256x300.jpg" alt="helo1" width="256" height="300" srcset="https://palmdoc.net/images/helo1-256x300.jpg 256w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo1-768x901.jpg 768w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo1-873x1024.jpg 873w" sizes="(max-width: 256px) 100vw, 256px"></p> 
        <p>For the medical people, don’t be put off by the “health stones” thrown in. Could be a gimmick but the meat of the package is the health band:</p> 
        <p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6806" src="http://palmdoc.net/images/helo3-289x300.jpg" alt="helo3" width="289" height="300" srcset="https://palmdoc.net/images/helo3-289x300.jpg 289w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo3-768x797.jpg 768w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo3-986x1024.jpg 986w" sizes="(max-width: 289px) 100vw, 289px"></p> 
        <p><img class="alignnone size-medium wp-image-6807" src="http://palmdoc.net/images/helo4-270x300.jpg" alt="helo4" width="270" height="300" srcset="https://palmdoc.net/images/helo4-270x300.jpg 270w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo4-768x854.jpg 768w, https://palmdoc.net/images/helo4-921x1024.jpg 921w" sizes="(max-width: 270px) 100vw, 270px"></p> 
        <p>There is a charging adaptor which you clip on to the back of the band, and a provided micro usb charging cable but no wall charger. You can use a standard usb wall charger or usb slot in your PC.</p> 
        <p>My first gripe is that the charging light indicator is rather tiny and it is difficult to ascertain if the light is green (fully charged) or red (charging) unless you dim the lights or shield the indicator with your hands.<br> The other thing is the charging adaptor is a little finnicky to put on and remove. My worry is if it would break if roughly handled. Hopefully a future design will address these two issues.</p> 
        <p><strong>First steps</strong><br> After charging up, you want to be able to sync with the HELO apps in your smartphone. There’s currently a beta HELO app for Android in the playstore and an iOS one which is installed using an Enterprise license. The links are <a href="http://www.worldgn.com/wear/helo-app" target="_blank">here</a></p> 
        <p>You power on the HELO by pressing on the side button for a couple of seconds. The light turns green when the device is turned on (again it’s a tiny light so it can be difficult to see unless you dim the surrounding light or shield the indicator with your hand). After you launch the HELO app it will ask you to create a free account (using email or mobile number) and then it will automatically look for the device and pair with it via Bluetooth. I initially had some pairing issues but figured out that the problem was that my Android Phone (a Xiaomi Redmi Note 3) was still paired with my Pebble Time watch. After disconnecting the Pebble the phone could pair with the HELO band without issues. After pairing, there was an immediate firmware update and subsequently I could use the HELO with my Android phone.</p>
