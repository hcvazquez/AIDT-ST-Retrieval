                    <span class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">At <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.ambiverse.com/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="ambiverse.com">Ambiverse</a></span> (where I am co-founder), we have a <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.ambiverse.com/natural-language-understanding-api/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="ambiverse.com">Natural Language Understanding API</a></span>, which identifies <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Named_entity" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org">named entities</a></span> in natural language text.</p><p class="qtext_para">The task is known as <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Entity_linking" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org">entity linking</a></span> and it consists of automatically identifying persons, locations, organizations, products, etc, mentioned in plain text. For instance, in the sentence "John talked to Yoko about his early days in Liverpool with Ringo, Paul and George" the idea is to understand that John refers to <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Lennon" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org">John Lennon</a></span>, Yoko to <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yoko_Ono" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org">Yoko Ono</a></span>, <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liverpool" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="wikipedia.org">Liverpool</a></span> to the English city and so on.</p><p class="qtext_para">Once the entities are identified the API provides the link to the wikipedia page of the entity plus a set of categories and descriptions. For example, John Lennon is classified as musician, as an English rock pianist, etc; and Liverpool is a city and a city port.</p><p class="qtext_para">You can try our <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.ambiverse.com/#demo" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="ambiverse.com">demo</a></span> to get a more concrete idea about entity linking or you can read our <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.ambiverse.com/who-is-mentioned-where-what-is-entity-linking/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="ambiverse.com">blog post</a></span> explaining entity linking in more detail. We also present some example uses of our API by analyzing the <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.ambiverse.com/oh-wie-schon-ist-offshore-panama/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="ambiverse.com">Panama Papers</a></span> and a <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.ambiverse.com/from-1914-to-1945-in-minutes-an-entity-centric-view-of-history/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 30630999);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="ambiverse.com">history book</a></span>.</p></span>

                    <div id="hrEXTA"></div>
                    <span class="rendered_qtext"><p class="qtext_para">There are already many good answers. Therefore, Im going to list libraries and APIs that havent been mentioned and I used.</p><p class="qtext_para">I use <span class="qlink_container"><a href="http://www.nltk.org/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 31437323);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="nltk.org">NLTK</a></span> (Natural Language Toolkit) for several projects and its pretty nice and easy to work with. Its currently maintained by Edinburgh University. I like it because its very complete. There are hundreds of natural language tools you can use for different tasks.</p><p class="qtext_para">Google has released a few trained NLP models for text summarisation, POS tagging, video captioning and more. The models publicly available <span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://github.com/tensorflow/models" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 31437323);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="github.com">tensorflow/models</a></span>. Some of this models are state-of-the-art but its quite complex to work with in comparison with NLTK for example.</p><p class="qtext_para">I have a side-project called <span class="qlink_container"><a href="http://nlpapi.co" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 31437323);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="nlpapi.co">NLProc API</a></span> that offers several tools for natural language processing: entity recognition, author extractor, summarisation, classification, main content extraction, full text rss feed, language detection, sentiment and question answering (soon). There are several SDKs for: Python, Java, Ruby, Javascript, .Net, PHP, Objective-c, Ruby or just plain curl.</p><p class="qtext_para">Its also open source<a id="cite-vWhPR" href="#vWhPR" class="citation_link">[1]</a> as long as youre happy to work with Python. Its an API and a library.</p><p class="qtext_citation_lead">Footnotes</p><p class="citation" id="vWhPR"><a href="#cite-vWhPR" class="citation_index">[1] </a><span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://github.com/webeng/feature_engineering" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 31437323);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="github.com">webeng/feature_engineering</a></span></p></span>
