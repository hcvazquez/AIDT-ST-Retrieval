        <div class="post-body__content">
         <p>Google Drive makes it painless to go paperless. Its collaborative documents, spreadsheets, and presentations already help curtail paper usage, but its OCR feature helps curb the paper mess even more.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>OCR, or Optical Character Recognition, is the most important tech to help you go paperless. Scanned documents on their own are only glorified pictures of your documents, but let your computer recognize the text and they instantly become a ton more useful. We've already looked at how to OCR documents in Adobe Acrobat:</p>
         <ul class="roundup-block__contents posts--half-width roundup-block--list">
          <li class="roundup-block__content"><a class="roundup-block__content-link" href="http://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/how-to-ocr-text-in-pdf-and-image-files-in-adobe-acrobat--cms-20406"><img class="roundup-block__preview-image" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/26/posts/20406/preview_image/adobe-acrobat-icon.jpg">
            <div class="roundup-block__primary-category topic-computers">
             Office
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__content-title">
             How to OCR Text in PDF and Image Files in Adobe Acrobat
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__author">
             Matthew Guay
            </div></a></li>
         </ul>
         <p> We've looked at turning PDF files into documents you can edit in Word as well:</p>
         <ul class="roundup-block__contents posts--half-width roundup-block--list">
          <li class="roundup-block__content"><a class="roundup-block__content-link" href="http://business.tutsplus.com/tutorials/how-to-edit-pdf-documents-in-microsoft-word--cms-20478"><img class="roundup-block__preview-image" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/26/posts/20478/preview_image/pdf-word.jpg">
            <div class="roundup-block__primary-category topic-business">
             Microsoft Word
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__content-title">
             How to Convert &amp; Edit PDF Documents in Microsoft Word
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__author">
             Matthew Guay
            </div></a></li>
         </ul>
         <p>Now if you don't have a copy of Acrobat or Word, there's an even better option: Google Drive. It includes a little-known free OCR tool that is a powerful, easy to use image to text converter.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>In this tutorial, we'll look at what is Google Drive's OCR process and simple steps to begin working with it.&nbsp;I'll show you how to use Google Drive&nbsp;to quickly convert your scanned images and PDF documents into editable text files online.<br></p> 
         <h2> <span class="sectionnum">1.</span> Scanning Your Documents for OCR</h2> 
         <p>The first step and most important step in OCR is finding the PDFs or pictures that you want to convert to text files. Google Drive currently supports OCR for .jpg, .gif, .png, and PDF files up to 2MB in size.&nbsp;</p> 
         <p><strong><em>Pro Tip:&nbsp;</em></strong><em>If you want to convert multiple pages to text, PDF format is the most efficient as all pages can be uploaded in one batch. </em><br></p> 
         <h3>Prepare Your Files Properly for OCR</h3> 
         <p>There are a few rules of thumb to produce optimal results from OCR in Google Drive. Make sure your file is high resolution with clear contrasts and even lightingthese are by far the most important factors in ensuring a successful conversion.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Use standard fonts to ensure the best OCR results" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/23/posts/20460/image/google-drive-ocr-image-to-text-best-practices.jpg">
          <figcaption>
           For best results, use a standard font like Helvetica. Stay away from overly stylized fonts or text effects.
          </figcaption>
         </figure> 
         <p>Additionally, make sure that the text being scanned is horizontal and reads from left to right. Standard typefaces, such as Helvetica and Times New Roman, will produce better results than more obscure typefaces. If you have a document that's rather unreadable, you can still attempt to OCR it, but the results likely won't be that nice.</p> 
         <p>While Google Drive supports OCR for many different languages, OCR on languages that use non-Latin character sets may be buggy and may not produce desirable results.</p>
         <h3>Gather Your Image Scans and PDF Files</h3> 
         <p>If your files are on your computer, make sure that their file format is one of those supported by Google Drive.&nbsp;If the document or photo you want to use is physical, then scan them onto the computer. There are a couple good options to use: &nbsp;</p>
         <ul> 
          <li>If you own a scanner, you can use programs like <a href="http://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/go-paperless-with-doxie--mac-47473" target="_self">Doxie</a> or other scanner software to digitize them for upload into Google Drive.</li> 
          <li>If you dont have a scanner, your phone can be used in place of one. There are a number of apps in the App Store that scan physical documents into clean PDFs using a phones camera.&nbsp;</li> 
         </ul>
         <p>For example, Evernote's <a href="https://evernote.com/products/scannable/">Scannable</a> is just one of several apps that allows you to scan documents with your smart phone. To learn more about Evernote, review the tutorial:</p>
         <ul class="roundup-block__contents posts--half-width roundup-block--list">
          <li class="roundup-block__content"><a class="roundup-block__content-link" href="http://computers.tutsplus.com/tutorials/turn-evernote-into-the-ultimate-paperless-system-with-scanned-pdfs--cms-20486"><img class="roundup-block__preview-image" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/26/posts/20486/preview_image/evernote-document-icon.jpg">
            <div class="roundup-block__primary-category topic-computers">
             Productivity
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__content-title">
             Turn Evernote Into the Ultimate Paperless System With Scanned PDFs
            </div> 
            <div class="roundup-block__author">
             Andrew Kunesh
            </div></a></li>
         </ul>
         <p>After taking your scan and transforming it into a clean PDF, your result should look something like this to start with:<br></p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="PDF document for OCR in Google Drive" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(1A).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           A PDF file as viewed in Adobe Acrobat Reader.
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p><span class="sectionnum">With your PDF or scanned image ready in the proper format, now let's look at how to get your files uploaded for OCR with Google Drive.&nbsp;</span></p> 
         <h2> <span class="sectionnum">2.</span> Upload Your Files to Google Drive for OCR</h2> 
         <p>It's a snap to convert your PDF and image files to text in Google Drive using it's OCR feature. Let's look at how to quickly do that.</p>
         <p>To add your documents to Google Drive to get them OCRed, first go in to your <a href="http://drive.google.com" target="_self">Google Drive</a> account. On the <strong>My Drive</strong> page, click the <strong>My Drive</strong> button next to the <strong>New</strong><strong></strong> button on the left side of the page. Select <strong>Upload Files</strong><strong></strong>.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Upload the files to Google Dive" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(2).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Use the Upload files option to upload a file to Google Drive.
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>Use the <b>Upload files </b>option to upload a file to Google Drive. Find the file that you want to convert from PDF or image to text. Click the <b>Open </b>button. <br></p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Upload the files to Google Drive" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(3).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Upload the files to Google Drive.
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>The document now appears in your Google Drive. Right-click on the document to bring up a drop-down menu.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Opening a document in your Google Drive" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(4).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           Open your file with Google Docs.
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>Click the <b>Open with</b> option and click <b>Google Docs</b>. A sheet icon appears while the file is downloading. Google is now in the process of converting your PDF or image file to text with OCR.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="Opening a file to OCR it" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(5).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           The file is downloading and being converted from PDF or picture to text.
          </figcaption>
          <br>
         </figure>
         <p>The file will open in Google Docs with the PDF converted to text, but with little formatting applied. You can now edit and format the new text file as you like.</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="The converted file after OCR" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(6).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           The converted file after OCR is applied in Google Drive comes out unformatted.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>Luckily, changes can easily be made due to the fact that the text is editable. You can use the same steps to OCR an image file using Google Drive as well.<br></p>
         <h3>Converting Images to Text in Google Drive</h3> 
         <p>You can quickly take your image scans into Google Drive to convert it to text using the same process as above.</p>
         <p>Here is a .jpg image of the text we've been working with before importing it into Google Drive:</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="jpg image before OCR in Google Drive" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(8).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           A .jpg image file before using Google Drive for OCR.
          </figcaption>
         </figure>
         <p>Here is the same picture file after we open it with Google Docs in Google Drive:</p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="jpg image after OCR process in Google Drive" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(9).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           .jpg image after OCR covernts it to text in Google Drive.
          </figcaption>
         </figure> 
         <p>Notice that the original image appears above the line. The editable text is beneath the line.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>Google Drive's OCR feature is powerful and easy to work with, you can quickly take an image scan or PDF and convert it into a text document. Once you've added your converted text file to Google Drive, now you have access to all the powerful document management features available.</p>
         <h2> <span class="sectionnum">3.</span> Managing OCR Documents in Google Drive</h2> 
         <p>One huge advantage to using OCR in Google Drive is that you can easily share the new document with whomever you need to. To do so, go to <strong>File &gt; Share</strong>, and you can add collaborators by sharing a link or sending an invitation via email. You can also click the <b>Share</b> button on the top right of the screen.</p>
         <p>Another way to manage your document after converting it with OCR is to export it to another format.<br></p>
         <figure class="post_image">
          <img alt="The OCR document can be exported as a different format" src="https://cms-assets.tutsplus.com/uploads/users/988/posts/20460/image/OCR%20Documents%20(10).jpg">
          <figcaption>
           The OCR document can be exported as a different format.
           <br>
          </figcaption>
         </figure> 
         <p>The OCR document may be exported as an editable text document, such as a Word Document or a Plain Text document, by going to <strong>File &gt; Download As</strong> and selecting the format you want.&nbsp;</p> 
         <h2>Now Get Working!</h2> 
         <p>Google Drive provides a quick and easy way to convert image and PDF files into editable text for free using its built-in OCR featue.&nbsp;</p>
         <p>These converted files can be easily shared or exported to different file formats for use at any time, and they're far more useful than your original scanned documents since you can now search through and copy text from everything you've scanned.</p>
         <p>If you have any trouble OCRing your documents in Google Drive, have any other OCR tools you love, or anything else you'd like to share about going paperless, let us know in the comments!</p>
