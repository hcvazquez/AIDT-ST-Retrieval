                 <div id="letter-box"> 
                  <div id="plan-a"> 
                   <h3 class="plan-a-color">What happens next?</h3> 
                   <p>We'll review your enrollment form to make sure it's complete and all eligibility requirements are met—and contact you if we need additional information. We'll then inform you by mail that we've received your form, and let Medicare know you've applied to join one of our plans.<br> Within 10 calendar days after Medicare confirms you're eligible, we'll first let you know the start date for your coverage, then we will send you a Kaiser Permanente ID card and your new member package.<br> <strong>Note: If you enrolled in a plan through the Marketplace, avoid being billed twice.</strong> As soon as your enrollment in our Medicare health plan is approved, remember to cancel the plan you purchased through the Marketplace. If you don't cancel your plan, you'll have to pay the premiums for both plans.</p> 
                  </div> 
                 </div> 
                 <div id="letter-box"> 
                  <div id="plan-b"> 
                   <h3 class="plan-b-color">3 things to do when you receive your Kaiser Permanente ID card</h3> 
                   <p><strong>Note:</strong> If you're a current Kaiser Permanente member joining our Medicare Health plan, these steps don't apply. You'll keep the care you know and trust.</p> 
                   <ol> 
                    <li><strong>Choose your Kaiser Permanente doctor</strong><br> Find the doctor that's right for you by browsing our online doctor profiles. And if your needs ever change, or it's just not the right fit, you can switch to another Kaiser Permanente doctor.</li> 
                    <li><strong>Register at kp.org</strong><br> As a Kaiser Permanente member, kp.org is your connection to tools and resources that help you manage your health. When you register, you can securely email your doctor's office, view lab results, schedule routine appointments, refill prescriptions, and more.*</li> 
                    <li><strong>Transfer your prescriptions</strong><br> If you need to refill an existing prescription before your first appointment, call our Member Service Contact Center. Please have your medical/health record number, prescription number(s), and previous pharmacy name and phone number handy. Prescription transfers can take two or more business days to process, so please plan ahead.</li> 
                   </ol> 
                   <p>For more information, visit <strong>kp.org/newmember</strong> to find out how to get started with Kaiser Permanente.</p> 
                   <p>*Some online services are not available in all regions.</p> 
                  </div>

                 <div id="letter-box"> 
                  <div id="plan-c"> 
                   <h3 class="plan-c-color">About payment options</h3> 
                   <p>For your convenience, we offer a variety of options to pay your monthly premium.</p> 
                   <p><strong>Social Security/Railroad Retirement Board</strong> <br> You may select to have your premium deducted from your Social Security or Railroad Retirement Board (RRB). This option may take two or more months to begin after Social Security or RRB approves the deduction. The first deduction may include all premiums due from your enrollment effective date—up to the point withholding begins. If this does not happen, we'll send you an invoice in the mail for the months before your deduction from Social Security or RRB check begins.</p> 
                   <p><strong>Electronic Funds Transfer</strong> <br> If you prefer, once you become a Kaiser Permanente member, you may choose to have your premium payment automatically deducted from your personal account. Complete the Electronic Funds Transfer Authorization form that applies to your state, and send it back to us. This will allow Kaiser Permanente to automatically withdraw your monthly premium from your account. After we receive your completed authorization form, please allow 30 days for processing.</p>
                   <p>Download the <a class="BodyLink" href="/medicare/pdfs/plans_current/ca_eft_form.pdf">Electronic Funds Transfer Authorization Form</a>? for California.</p> 
                   <p>Download the <a class="BodyLink" href="/medicare/pdfs/plans_current/roc_eft_form.pdf">Electronic Funds Transfer Authorization Form</a>? for Colorado, Georgia, Hawaii, Oregon, and Washington.</p> 
                   <p>Download the <a class="BodyLink" href="/medicare/pdfs/plans_current/mas_eft_form.pdf">Electronic Funds Transfer Authorization Form</a>? for Maryland, Virginia and Washington D.C.</p> 
                   <p>Please note, if you are enrolled in automatic deductions from Social Security or RRB, and you enroll in Electronic Funds Transfer, your premium deduction from Social Security or RRB will automatically stop. </p> 
                   <p><strong>Monthly Bill</strong><br> You can choose to receive a monthly bill from Kaiser Permanente and pay your plan premium by mail each month.</p>
