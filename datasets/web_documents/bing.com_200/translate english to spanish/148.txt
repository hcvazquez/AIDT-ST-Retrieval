           <section data-src="hm">
            <h2>trans·late</h2> 
            <span class="snd" data-snd="T0320100"></span>&nbsp;
            <span class="pron" onclick="pron_key()">(tr?ns?l?t?, tr?nz?-, tr?ns-l?t?, tr?nz-)</span>
            <div class="pseg">
             <i>v.</i> 
             <b>trans·lat·ed</b>, 
             <b>trans·lat·ing</b>, 
             <b>trans·lates</b> 
            </div>
            <div class="pseg">
             <i>v.</i>
             <i>tr.</i>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>1. </b> To render in another language: 
              <span class="illustration">translated the Korean novel into German.</span>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>2. </b> To express in different, often simpler words: 
              <span class="illustration">translated the technical jargon into ordinary language.</span>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>3. </b>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>a. </b> To change from one form, function, or state to another; convert or transform: 
               <span class="illustration">translate ideas into reality.</span>
              </div>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>b. </b> To express in another medium: 
               <span class="illustration">translated the short story into a movie.</span>
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>4. </b> To transfer from one place or condition to another: 
              <span class="illustration">"His remains were translated to San Juan de Puerto Rico where they still rest"</span> 
              <span class="illustration">(Samuel Eliot Morison).</span>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>5. </b> To forward or retransmit (a telegraphic message).
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>6. </b>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>a. </b> 
               <i>Ecclesiastical</i> To transfer (a bishop) to another see.
              </div>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>b. </b> To convey to heaven without death.
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>7. </b> 
              <i>Physics</i> To subject (a body) to translation.
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>8. </b> 
              <i>Biology</i> To subject (messenger RNA) to translation.
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="pseg">
             <i>v.</i>
             <i>intr.</i>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>1. </b>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>a. </b> To make a translation.
              </div>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>b. </b> To work as a translator.
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>2. </b> To admit of translation: 
              <span class="illustration">His poetry translates well.</span>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>3. </b> To be changed or transformed in effect. Often used with 
              <i>into</i> or 
              <i>to</i>: 
              <span class="illustration">"Today's low inflation and steady growth in household income translate into more purchasing power"</span> 
              <span class="illustration">(Thomas G. Exter).</span>
             </div>
            </div>

           <section data-src="hc_dict">
            <h2>translate</h2> (
            <span onclick="pron_key(1)" class="pron">træns?le?t; trænz-</span>) 
            <div>
             <i>vb</i>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>1. </b>to express or be capable of being expressed in another language or dialect: 
              <span class="illustration">he translated Shakespeare into Afrikaans</span>; 
              <span class="illustration">his books translate well</span>. 
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>2. </b> (
              <i>intr</i>) to act as translator
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>3. </b> (
              <i>tr</i>) to express or explain in simple or less technical language
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>4. </b> (
              <i>tr</i>) to interpret or infer the significance of (gestures, symbols, etc)
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>5. </b> (
              <i>tr</i>) to transform or convert: 
              <span class="illustration">to translate hope into reality</span>. 
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>6. </b> (Biochemistry) (
              <i>tr; usually passive</i>) 
              <i>biochem</i> to transform the molecular structure of (messenger RNA) into a polypeptide chain by means of the information stored in the genetic code. See also 
              <a href="transcribe">transcribe</a>
              <sup>7</sup>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>7. </b>to move or carry from one place or position to another
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>8. </b> (Ecclesiastical Terms) (
              <i>tr</i>) 
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>a. </b>to transfer (a cleric) from one ecclesiastical office to another
              </div>
              <div class="sds-list">
               <b>b. </b>to transfer (a see) from one place to another
              </div>
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>9. </b> (Roman Catholic Church) (
              <i>tr</i>) 
              <i>RC Church</i> to transfer (the body or the relics of a saint) from one resting place to another
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>10. </b> (Theology) (
              <i>tr</i>) 
              <i>theol</i> to transfer (a person) from one place or plane of existence to another, as from earth to heaven
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>11. </b> (General Physics) 
              <i>maths</i> 
              <i>physics</i> to move (a figure or body) laterally, without rotation, dilation, or angular displacement
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>12. </b> (Aeronautics) (
              <i>intr</i>) (of an aircraft, missile, etc) to fly or move from one position to another
             </div>
             <div class="ds-list">
              <b>13. </b> (
              <i>tr</i>) 
              <i>archaic</i> to bring to a state of spiritual or emotional ecstasy
             </div>
            </div>
            <div class="etyseg">
             [C13: from Latin 
             <i>transl?tus</i> transferred, carried over, from
