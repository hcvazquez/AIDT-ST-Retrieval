      <td valign="top" colspan="2"><h2>Creating Photo Objects by Removing Background From Images</h2> <p>In this tutorial, the image background will be removed using the <b> Exract</b> filter in the Adobe® Photoshop® CS3®. Please note that the <b> Extract</b> filter is not installed in Adobe® Photoshop® CS4®, therefore, you have to download the plug-in <a href="http://www.adobe.com/support/downloads/detail.jsp?ftpID=4048">for Windows</a> or <a href="http://www.adobe.com/support/downloads/detail.jsp?ftpID=4047">for Mac OS</a> and install it manually.</p> <p>You can also download the following <a href="/media/p4/images/tutorial/source_image.jpg" target="new">sample image</a> to be used with this tutorial.</p> <p>We will use a mask to hide the white background&nbsp;from the&nbsp;image. In the tutorial, we will remove the solid background color.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> </td>

      <td valign="top"><b>Step 1.</b> Open an image with the background you would like to remove. <p>&nbsp;</p> <p>A higher image resolution makes it easier to work on an image. It also reduces the visual defects that might appear on contour lines after the image downsizing.</p> <p>&nbsp;</p> </td>

        <li>Set the <b>Tolerance</b> to 25-30.</li>

      <td><img src="/media/p4/images/tutorial/image005.png" alt=""><br><img src="/media/p4/images/tutorial/image006.jpg" alt=""><br><img src="/media/p4/images/tutorial/image007.jpg" alt=""></td>

        <li>Use the <b>Paint Bucket</b> tool to fill the new layer with <b>black</b>.</li>

          <li>Use the <b>Eraser</b> tool, or press <b>&lt;E&gt;</b>, to clear the highlight.</li>

   <a name="used_terms"></a>
   <h2>Used Terms</h2> 
   <p>The <b>Selection</b> tools allow selecting a portion or a subset of an image to work on. Most of tools and filters can be applied to this selected area to alter colors, shape, position and other attributes, leaving the rest of the image untouched.</p> 
   <br> 
   <p><img src="/media/p4/images/tutorial/image048.png" alt=""></p> 
   <br> 
   <p><b>Masks</b> are useful for hiding and blending parts of an image without modifying it directly. You can fix the edges of the selection by painting with black to add to the mask or painting with white to show more of the image. The transparency is achieved by changing the brush opacity.</p> 
   <p><img src="/media/p4/images/tutorial/image049.png" alt=""></p>
