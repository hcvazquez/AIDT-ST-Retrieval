         <strong>Note: </strong>reCAPTCHA is currently only available for Form Builder 4.4.0 and higher. If you have not upgraded to Form Builder 4.4.0 then you will not be able to use reCAPTCHA and your form will only be able to show the traditional CAPTCHA.

        <h3>About the reCAPTCHA</h3> 
        <p><br> A <a href="https://developers.google.com/recaptcha/">Google reCAPTCHA</a> is a test used to determine if the person submitting the form is a real human, or an automated program (bot). While this technique is not 100% foolproof, it should cut down the spam you receive quite significantly. &nbsp;It is also an improvement over the traditional CAPTCHA, where users can sometimes have difficulty differentiating letters.&nbsp;</p> 
        <p>Example:</p> 
        <p class="center"><img src="//dyzz9obi78pm5.cloudfront.net/app/image/id/5836fb0f32131c5953aed963/n/screen-shot-2016-11-24-at-9.36" style="height: 152px; width: 600px;"></p> 
        <p class="center" style="text-align: left;">Your respondents will also need to have JavaScript enabled on their browser in order to submit a form using&nbsp;ReCaptcha. &nbsp;If they do not have JavaScript enabled, they will see a pop-up error when they try to submit the form. This can also be beneficial for ensuring that users have completed all required fields, as FormAssembly's validation rules also require JavaScript to be in enabled in order to run.<br> &nbsp;</p> 
        <hr> 
        <h3>Enable the reCAPTCHA</h3> 
        <p>To enable the reCAPTCHA on one of your forms, scroll to the bottom of the <strong>Processing</strong>&nbsp;page.&nbsp;<span style="line-height: 1.428571429;">Check the checkbox to add a reCAPTCHA&nbsp;</span><span style="line-height: 1.428571429;">to your form.</span></p> 
        <hr> 
        <h3>Errors with reCAPTCHA</h3> 
        <p>In rare cases, an error might occur with Google's reCAPTCHA. If this is the case, our older CAPTCHA option will be automatically initiated. You and your users will not notice any difference, other than the old CAPTCHA taking the place of the new reCAPTCHA.</p>
