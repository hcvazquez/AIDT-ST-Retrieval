                        <h3><a name="TOC-Quick-Sort"></a><font size="3"><b>Quick Sort</b></font></h3>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3"><br></font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">Quick Sort is divide and conquer algorithm like Merge Sort. Unlike Merge Sort this does not&nbsp;</font>
                         <span style="font-size:medium">require extra space. So it sorts in place. Here dividing step is to chose a pivot and partition the&nbsp;</span>
                         <span style="font-size:medium">array such that all elements less than or equal to pivot are to the left of it and all the elements&nbsp;</span>
                         <span style="font-size:medium">which are greater than or equal to the pivot are to the right of it. Recursively sort the left and&nbsp;</span>
                         <span style="font-size:medium">right parts.</span>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3"><br></font>
                        </div>
                        <h3><a name="TOC-Algorithm-described-in-detail-in-the-document-for-this-tutorial-"></a><font size="3"><b>Algorithm (described in detail in the document for this tutorial)</b></font></h3>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3"><br></font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">The key to the algorithm is the partition procedure.</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">A 'partition' element is chosen. All elements less than the partition are put in the left half of the array, all elements greater than the partition are placed in the right half of the array.</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">The two halves are sorted independently and recursively.</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3"><br></font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3"><span style="line-height:24px"><br></span></font>
                        </div>
                        <h3><a name="TOC-Property:"></a><b style="font-size:medium">Property:</b></h3>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3"><br></font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">1. Best case performance  When the partitioning produces two regions of size n/2 (where,</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">n is the total number of elements in the list) O(nlgn).</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">2. Worst case performance - When the partitioning produces one region of size n-1 (where,</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">n is the total number of elements in the list) and other of size 1 O(n2).</font>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <span style="font-size:medium">3. Average case  O(n(lgn))</span>
                        </div>
                        <div>
                         <font size="3">4. It is not stable and uses O(lg(n)) extra space in the worst case.</font>
                        </div>
