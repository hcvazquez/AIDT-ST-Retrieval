     <h1>The MARY Text-to-Speech System (MaryTTS)</h1> 
     <p>MaryTTS is an open-source, multilingual Text-to-Speech Synthesis platform written in Java. It was originally developed as a collaborative project of <a class="externalLink" href="http://www.dfki.de/web">DFKI</a>’s <a class="externalLink" href="http://www.dfki.de/lt/">Language Technology Lab</a> and the <a class="externalLink" href="http://www.coli.uni-saarland.de/groups/WB/Phonetics/">Institute of Phonetics</a> at <a class="externalLink" href="http://www.uni-saarland.de">Saarland University</a>. It is now maintained by the <a class="externalLink" href="http://m2ci.org/en/irg/msp">Multimodal Speech Processing</a> Group in the <a class="externalLink" href="http://m2ci.org/">Cluster of Excellence MMCI</a> and DFKI.</p> 
     <p>As of version 5.2, MaryTTS supports German, British and American English, French, Italian, Luxembourgish, Russian, Swedish, Telugu, and Turkish; more languages are in preparation. MaryTTS comes with toolkits for quickly <a class="externalLink" href="https://github.com/marytts/marytts/wiki/New-Language-Support">adding support for new languages</a> and for building <a class="externalLink" href="https://github.com/marytts/marytts/wiki/VoiceImportToolsTutorial">unit selection</a> and <a class="externalLink" href="https://github.com/marytts/marytts/wiki/HMMVoiceCreation">HMM-based synthesis voices</a>.</p>
