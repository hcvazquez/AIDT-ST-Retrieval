            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>The only way to check if</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li>the address is valid</li> 
               <li>the mailbox is working</li> 
               <li>the email was given by the owner</li> 
              </ul> 
              <p>is to send a confirmation email with a link and a unique token. The recipient will then click on the link and confirm that the he got the email.</p> 
              <p>Summarized:</p> 
              <ol> 
               <li>Generate a <strong>unique</strong> ID and store it along with the hashed supplied email address</li> 
               <li>Send an email to the address with a URL to your validation application/script (e.g., <a href="https://example.com/validate/id=UNIQUEID&amp;email=HASHEDEMAIL" rel="nofollow">https://example.com/validate/id=UNIQUEID&amp;email=HASHEDEMAIL</a>)</li> 
               <li>When the URL is clicked your application will check if the unique ID corresponds to what you generated and sent to the user</li> 
              </ol>

             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>I have done this in the past in PHP using SwiftMailer, but you could probably achieve the same in straight php.</p> 
              <p>If you send your email with a from address like <code>no-reply@mydomain.com</code> (which is a real address to which you have access) the email will be sent back to that box.</p> 
              <p>From there, you can have a planned task to check for emails in that box, grab the email to which you sent it and flag de user as a user with an invalid address and don't send anymore communication to them. After processing the email, you could either delete the email in the box or store it in an other folder.</p> 
              <p>When I did it, I an a requirement to keep a copy of every email sent by the application. To identify every unsuccessful deliveries, I added a unique key to the email headers which was grabed back by the code analysing the rebound mailbox and I could flag every correspondances sent (ex: 2 first message were successfully delivered, but the 3rd and 4th were not)</p>
