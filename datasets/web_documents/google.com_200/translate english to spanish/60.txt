        <div class="content-list-component bn-content-list-text text">
         <p>This earpiece is a real smooth talker.</p>
        </div> 
        <div class="content-list-component bn-content-list-text text">
         <p>Waverly Labs has created a device called <a href="http://www.waverlylabs.com/" target="_blank">The Pilot System</a>, which translates English, French, Spanish and Italian in real time. This means when someone is speaking to you in any of these languages and you dont understand what they are saying, all you have to do is put the device in your ear and itll translate what that person is saying as they say it. Its like Babel fish from&nbsp;<em>Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy</em>&nbsp;IRL.</p>
        </div> 
        <figure class="bn-content-list-image content-list-component image"> 
         <img class="image__src" src="https://img.huffingtonpost.com/asset/573ceb7713000001053821fc.png?ops=scalefit_720_noupscale">

            The device comes in three colors.&nbsp; 
          </figcaption>

        <div class="content-list-component bn-content-list-text text">
         <p>The gadget, which will retail for $299, includes a second earpiece, which can be used for wireless music streaming, and a battery charger. The user must also download an app onto their phone in order to toggle languages in the earpiece. Additional downloadable language packs for Slavic, Semitic, Hindi and East Asian languages will also be available.</p>
        </div> 
        <div class="content-list-component bn-content-list-text text">
         <p>You can now <a href="http://www.waverlylabs.com/launch/" target="_blank">pre-order</a> The Pilot, but the whole kit and caboodle  meaning the app and device  wont be available as a complete package until Spring 2017.</p>

According to <a href="https://apps.mla.org/pdf/2013_enrollment_survey.pdf" target="_blank">a report</a> published by the Modern Language Association, the number of language enrollments in higher education in the U.S. dropped by 6.7 percent between 2009 and 2013 after increasingly steadily since 1995. Translation? Roughly <a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2015/02/19/americans-are-beginning-to-lose-their-love-for-foreign-languages/" target="_blank">100,000 fewer</a> students enrolled in language classes in American colleges.</p>
