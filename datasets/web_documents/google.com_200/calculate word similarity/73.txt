             <div class="abstract author" id="ab010" lang="en" data-reactid="136">
              <div id="as015" data-reactid="137">
               <h3 id="st145" data-reactid="138">Introduction</h3>
               <p id="sp015" data-reactid="139">In this article, we evaluate a knowledge-based word sense disambiguation method that determines the intended concept associated with an ambiguous word in biomedical text using semantic similarity and relatedness measures. These measures quantify the degree of similarity or relatedness between concepts in the Unified Medical Language System (UMLS). The objective of this work is to develop a method that can disambiguate terms in biomedical text by exploiting similarity and relatedness information extracted from biomedical resources and to evaluate the efficacy of these measure on WSD.</p>
              </div>
              <div id="as020" data-reactid="140">
               <h3 id="st150" data-reactid="141">Method</h3>
               <p id="sp020" data-reactid="142">We evaluate our method on a biomedical dataset (MSH-WSD) that contains 203 ambiguous terms and acronyms.</p>
              </div>
              <div id="as025" data-reactid="143">
               <h3 id="st155" data-reactid="144">Results</h3>
               <p id="sp025" data-reactid="145">We show that information content-based measures derived from either a corpus or taxonomy obtain a higher disambiguation accuracy than path-based measures or relatedness measures on the MSH-WSD dataset.</p>
              </div>
              <div id="as030" data-reactid="146">
