     <h2 class="heading">A website is telling me to disable AdBlock before it will show content <a href="javascript:print();" class="icon-print solution-print-icon" title="Print this Article"></a> </h2> 
     <div>
       Created by: Rhana Cassidy 
     </div> 
     <p>Modified on: Thu, 31 Aug, 2017 at 11:49 AM</p> 
     <hr> 
     <article class="article-body" id="article-body" rel="image-enlarge"> 
      <p><span style="font-size: 13px; background-color: transparent; text-align: initial;">More and more websites are asking (or sometimes requiring) visitors to turn off ad blockers before allowing them to view the site's contents. It's sometimes possible to prevent sites from detecting you're using an ad blocker (see "Turn off JavaScript," below); but doing so can cause parts of the site to stop working. Fortunately, it isn't necessary. There are ways to get to the site's content without having to turn off your ad blocker, although some of them do allow ads to appear.</span></p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p><strong>Note:</strong> We are not affiliated with, nor do we have any control or influence over, the developers of the software and websites mentioned in this article. We offer them as a service to our users. If you need help, please contact the developers directly.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p><strong>Wait, I'm on a mobile device!</strong></p>
      <p>The suggestions below work in your desktop browser. Things work a little differently if you use AdBlock on mobile devices. You can <a href="https://help.getadblock.com/solution/articles/6000111843" target="_blank">pause AdBlock on your mobile device</a> or <a href="https://help.getadblock.com/solution/articles/6000151096-can-i-whitelist-a-site-in-adblock-on-my-iphone-or-ipad-" target="_blank">whitelist websites on your iPhone or iPad</a>.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h1>I like the site and I want to support it by allowing ads</h1>
      <p>If the site is one you enjoy and visit frequently and the ads it serves are nonintrusive, we encourage you to <a href="https://help.getadblock.com/solution/articles/6000055743-how-do-i-tell-adblock-not-to-block-ads-" target="_blank">whitelist the site</a>.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h1>I don't mind ads but I don't want to whitelist the site&nbsp;</h1>
      <p>To stop AdBlock from blocking ads temporarily, click the <strong>AdBlock button</strong> in the browser toolbar and select <strong>Pause AdBlock</strong>. This pauses ad blocking on every website, not just the one you're visiting, so remember to unpause AdBlock the same way when you leave the site.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h1>I want to get my content without ads</h1>
      <p>The following methods will allow you to view content on many anti-adblocking sites without having to pause AdBlock or whitelist the site. Depending on which method you use, you may encounter sites where it isn't feasible to bypass anti-adblocking <em>and</em> also block ads.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h2>Employ userscripts to thwart anti-adblocking sites</h2>
      <p><span style="font-size: 13px; background-color: transparent; text-align: initial;">Quick review: Userscripts are tiny bits of computer code that help your browser do extra things. Your browser needs a script manager to run userscripts. Tampermonkey and Greasemonkey are the most popular script managers.</span></p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>Step 1: On the FILTER LISTS tab in AdBlock's options, unsubscribe from the Acceptable Ads filter list and subscribe to the Adblock Warning Removal list.</p>
      <p>Step 2: Install the Tampermonkey or Greasemonkey script manager appropriate for your browser.</p>
      <p>Step 3: Install the AAK-Cont userscript.</p>
      <p>Step 4: Subscribe to the AAK-Cont filter list.</p>
      <p>Step 5: Install the It's Not Important userscript.</p>
      <p>Step 6: Add custom filters (<em>only</em> if needed for a particular website and <em>only if you consider yourself an advanced user; see the warning below</em>).</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>Steps 2 through 5: You'll find links to Tampermonkey, Greasemonkey, the AAK-Cont userscript, and the AAK-Cont filter list on the <a href="https://gitlab.com/xuhaiyang1234/AAK-Cont#installation-instruction" target="_blank">AAK-Cont instructions page</a>. Here's where to get the <a href="https://greasyfork.org/en/scripts/14720-it-s-not-important" target="_blank">It's Not Important userscript</a>.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>Step 6: <strong><span style="color: rgb(184, 49, 47);">Warning:</span></strong><span style="color: rgb(184, 49, 47);"><strong>&nbsp;Custom filters can break websites!</strong>&nbsp;</span><em><span style="color: rgb(184, 49, 47);">Please read these <a href="https://help.getadblock.com/solution/articles/6000161377-how-to-use-custom-filters" target="_blank"><strong>important warnings</strong></a> before you attempt to use them.</span></em></p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>Add this custom filter on the CUSTOMIZE tab in AdBlock's options, replacing "domain.com" with the domain of the site you're visiting: @@||domain.com$generichide</p>
      <p>(Replace "generichide" with "elemhide" in Safari and Opera.)</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>If a website still forces you to disable AdBlock, it may need additional custom filters that aren't included in the AAK-Cont list. You can often find some by asking in the <a href="https://forums.lanik.us/index.php" target="_blank">EasyList forums</a>. We may also have some in the section <a href="https://help.getadblock.com/solution/categories/6000128238" target="_blank"><em>for advanced users only</em></a> in our Knowledge Base.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p><strong>Notes:</strong>&nbsp;</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p><span style="font-size: 13px; background-color: transparent; text-align: initial;">Anti-Adblock Killer Continued (AAK-Cont) is a continuation of a project that was abandoned by the original developer. AAK-Cont is not guaranteed to work on all sites. Please report problems or non-working sites in&nbsp;</span><a href="https://gitlab.com/xuhaiyang1234/AAK-Cont/issues" style="font-size: 13px; text-align: initial;" target="_blank">the AAK-Cont issues tracker</a><span style="font-size: 13px; background-color: transparent; text-align: initial;">. Be patient; the folks maintaining the project are volunteers and may not be able to get to your issue right away.</span></p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>The method presented above works with AdBlock and Adblock Plus in any browser and with µBlock Origin in any browser except Chrome. If you use Chrome and µBlock Origin, use this <a href="https://jspenguin2017.github.io/uBlockProtector/" target="_blank">alternate method</a>.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h2>View a cached version of the page</h2>
      <p>If the method suggested above seems too intimidating, don't use it. You have alternatives that may meet your needs with less work on your part.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>You can search for a cached version of the page, which is an archived "snapshot" of the live page. Cached pages don't ask you to disable your ad blocker, and often don't include the ads that appear on the live page. (Thank AdBlock fan @OrialiRf for this idea.) The two most popular search engines for cached pages are <a href="http://www.googleguide.com/cached_pages.html" target="_blank">Google Cache</a> and the <a href="https://archive.org/web/" target="_blank">Internet Archive</a>, fondly known as the Wayback Machine.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p>Browser extensions make this even easier by putting a friendlier interface between you, Google Cache, and the Wayback Machine. (Shout out to Gabriel for this suggestion.) Chrome users can try <a href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/web-cache-viewer/pbkloffickinnlnmefmjmjbacohecpbd" target="_blank">Web Cache Viewer</a>. Firefox users can try <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/cacheit/" target="_blank">CacheIt!</a> or <a href="https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox/addon/resurrect-pages/" target="_blank">Resurrect Pages</a>. We couldn't find similar extensions for Microsoft Edge and Safari. If you know of any, please tell us in the feedback for this article!</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p><strong>Note:</strong> This method won't help you on WSJ.com. The Wall Street Journal no longer allows search engines to cache pages on its website. Other sites may start doing that as well, so no guarantees where or for how long this workaround will continue to work.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h2>Use your browser's "reading mode"</h2>
      <p>View the page in your browser's reading mode, if it has one, to strip out ads, background images, buttons, and other clutter. Enter the name of your browser and "reading mode" in your favorite search engine to learn how to turn on reading mode.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h2 style="line-height: 25.0965px;">Turn off JavaScript</h2>
      <p>Many sites use JavaScript to fetch ads and to test for the presence of ad blockers. Try disabling JavaScript in your browser to prevent that code from running. Enter "disable JavaScript" and the name of your browser in your favorite search engine to learn how to turn off JavaScript. <strong><span style="color: rgb(184, 49, 47);">Warning:</span></strong><span style="color: rgb(184, 49, 47);">&nbsp;Disabling JavaScript can break websites.</span> If all you want is to read an article, though, you probably won't notice any missing functionality.</p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h1>How does a website know you're using an ad blocker, anyway?</h1>
      <p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">There are a couple of ways websites can detect ad blockers. The easiest way is to try to download a tiny piece of JavaScript code that's like "bait" for an ad blocker, then use another piece of code to test whether the download occurred. If the download is blocked, the site knows an ad blocker is in use.</span></p>
      <p><br></p>
      <p><span style="font-family: arial, helvetica, sans-serif;">Similarly, the site can try to download a resource that's commonly used to serve an ad, and if that resource is blocked or hidden, it indicates an ad blocker is present.</span></p>
      <p><br></p>
      <h1><span style="color: inherit; font-size: 1.17em; font-weight: 400; background-color: transparent; text-align: initial;">Did you disable AdBlock but the site still tells you to turn off your ad blocker?</span></h1>
      <p>If you have paused AdBlock, whitelisted the website, or don't even have an ad blocker, but you're still seeing that "please disable your ad blocker" warning, it means there may be something in your browser that is causing the site's ad block detection test to generate a false positive. We have <a href="https://help.getadblock.com/solution/articles/6000161397-i-turned-off-adblock-but-a-site-still-asks-me-to-turn-off-my-ad-blocker" target="_blank">some troubleshooting steps to try</a>.</p>
