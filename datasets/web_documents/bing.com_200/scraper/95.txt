With basic elements, simply highlighting, right-clicking and selecting scrape similar from the context menu is all you need to do. From there you can export to google docs (super handy!) or just copy to clipboard. For more complex scraping, it supports both jquery and x-path (come to think of it, this is more or less what helped me get started learning both!). A must have.</p><p class="qtext_para"><span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://import.io/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 39345094);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="import.io">Import.io</a></span></p><p class="qtext_para">Although it isnt free, they offer a free trial (and dont seem too particular about doing so multiple times). <span class="qlink_container"><a href="http://Import.io" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 39345094);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="import.io">Import.io | Extract data from the web</a></span> is great for crawling or data extraction. Its web-based foundations lets you create APIs for reuse or for integrating into almost any other application that supports API. Moreover, its very friendly towards non-programmers, and has a variety of ways to select and acquire the data you are looking to scrape. It also supports Java, and also boasts the ability to login prior to a scrape (for protected content). Awesome tool, I just wish it didnt come at such a steep price after the trial is up!</p><p class="qtext_para"><span class="qlink_container"><a href="http://www.screen-scraper.com/technology.php" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 39345094);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="screen-scraper.com">Screen-Scraper</a></span></p><p class="qtext_para">Screen-Scraper handles beginner to moderate extraction like a boss, but performs a few handy automation tasks (like logging in, filling forms etc), making it great for scripted scraping. I dont have a lot of experience with it, but I keep seeing it pop up in reviews and general use cases. Worth a look for sure.</p><p class="qtext_para"><span class="qlink_container"><a href="https://www.outwit.com/products/hub/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 39345094);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="outwit.com">Outwit Hub</a></span></p><p class="qtext_para">A mainstay for years, Outwit Hub is extremely powerful (even in its free version!), but is more complex than you might be looking for. Some users find its GUI and layout very intuitive and get the hang of it very quickly though, so dont immediately get discouraged. Outwit has been around for as long as Ive been scraping, and has built up one heck of a reputation. If you are feeling adventurous, Id give it a shot first things first.</p><p class="qtext_para"><span class="qlink_container"><a href="http://80legs.com/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 39345094);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="80legs.com">80legs</a></span></p><p class="qtext_para">80legs is great for beginners, if you arent too particular about grabbing ONLY specific data, or if your needs are limited. This one is a bit difficult to explain, just give it a look.</p><p class="qtext_para"><span class="qlink_container"><a href="http://www.octoparse.com/" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank" onclick="return Q.openUrl(this, 39345094);" class="external_link" data-qt-tooltip="octoparse.com">OctoParse</a></span></p><p class="qtext_para">If youre new to scraping, this might be your go-to. The OctoParse software is outstanding, and makes scraping a relatively pain-free process. It also features a reliable automation engine as well as proxy support (cloud-based scraping functionality is available, but not free, GREAT for massive scrape projects). Aside from its point-and-click UI, it also supports Java, AJAX, jquery, xpath and a few more, allowing for precise scrapes. I strongly suggest you start here. Although there is a learning process, its probably the easiest starting point.</p><p class="qtext_para">Scraping techniques are based largely on the tool(s) youve decided to use, and since you havent specified potential targets or types of data you are looking to extract, this is as far as this answer goes.
