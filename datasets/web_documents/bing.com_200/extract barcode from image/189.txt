              <p>Did you try something simple like correlation?</p> 
              <p>(<strong>EDIT</strong>). The idea behind correlation is to use a template (in your case a trained road sign sample), and compare it to every position in the test image. The comparison operation I've used to generate the images below is called normalized <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross-correlation#Normalized_cross-correlation" rel="noreferrer">cross-correlation</a>. Roughly speaking, you standardize (mean=0, standard deviation=1) the pixels in the template and the image part you want to match, multiply them pixel by pixel, and calculate the mean value of the products. This way you get a "match score", i.e. a measure of similarity between the template and the test image at every position in the test image. The position with the best match (highest correlation) is the most probably candidate for the position of the road sign. (Actually, I've used the Mathematica function <a href="http://reference.wolfram.com/mathematica/ref/CorrelationDistance.html" rel="noreferrer">CorrelationDistance</a> to generate the image below, which is 1 - (normalized correlation). So the darkest spot in the match image corresponds to the best match). </p> 
              <p>I don't have any other templates, so I simple cropped the sign from the second picture you posted:</p> 
              <p><img src="https://i.stack.imgur.com/mECcU.png" alt="enter image description here"></p> 
              <p>Even though the template is rotated slightly, cross correlation still looks usable</p> 
              <p><img src="https://i.stack.imgur.com/MH9pw.png" alt="enter image description here"></p> 
              <p>and the best match is found at the right position:</p> 
              <p><img src="https://i.stack.imgur.com/z9cq2.png" alt="enter image description here"></p> 
              <p>(You'd need multiple scaled versions of each template to detect signs at any size, of course)</p>

            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>During my Masters, the project my supervisor was involved in was dealing in detecting and recognizing all kinds of different traffic signalization in a video sequences (e.g. road detection, road centerline detection, but also <strong>traffic sign detection and recognition</strong>). The video frames we were working on are in many ways similar to your example images.</p> 
              <p>While I personally didn't work on traffic signs, I think the best results were obtained by using the <a href="http://www.google.com/url?sa=t&amp;rct=j&amp;q=&amp;esrc=s&amp;source=web&amp;cd=1&amp;cad=rja&amp;ved=0CCkQFjAA&amp;url=http%3A%2F%2Fresearch.microsoft.com%2F~viola%2FPubs%2FDetect%2FviolaJones_IJCV.pdf&amp;ei=D3iKUP3OJYix0QXNx4G4Cw&amp;usg=AFQjCNHv5ZMXrBGA2ETaBg6suywybnkoQg"><strong>Viola-Jones Algorithm</strong> (paper)</a>. In short, it is an algorithm that uses a cascade of <em>weak classifiers</em> (with accuracy just a bit higher than that of a random algorithm) to construct a <em>strong classifier</em> that is robust even in difficult tasks.</p> 
              <p>The project was called <a href="http://www.zemris.fer.hr/~ssegvic/mastif/index_en.shtml">MASTIF (Mapping and Assessing the State of Traffic InFrastructure)</a> and did some really good work. Project's <a href="http://www.zemris.fer.hr/~ssegvic/mastif/publications_en.shtml">publication page</a> could be really useful because it provides links to all the published papers related to the project. Just to give you an idea, let me single some of the publications out (in chronological order):</p> 
              <ul> 
               <li><a href="http://www.zemris.fer.hr/~ssegvic/pubs/oagm09.pdf">Karla Brki?, Axel Pinz, Siniša Šegvi?: "Traffic sign detection as a component of an automated traffic infrastructure inventory system"</a></li> 
               <li><a href="http://www.zemris.fer.hr/~ssegvic/mastif/pubs/martinovic10.pdf">A. Martinovi?, G. Glavaš, M. Juribaši?, D. Suti? and Z. Kalafati?: "Real-time Detection and Recognition of Traffic Signs"</a></li> 
               <li><a href="http://www.zemris.fer.hr/~ssegvic/pubs/bonaci11cvww.pdf">Igor Bona?i, Ivan Kusali?, Ivan Kova?ek, Zoran Kalafati?, Siniša Šegvi?: "Addressing false alarms and localization inaccuracy in traffic sign detection and recognition"</a></li> 
              </ul> 
              <p>Once again, I didn't personally work on traffic signs, but I think you can find plenty of useful material here. Also, I would suggest going through cited references in the papers as they can be of help as well.</p>

            <td class="answercell"> 
             <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
              <p>I'm definitely not an expert here but you can start with edge detection (such as canny), then hough transform to detect circles, rectangles, squares, or triangles (based on the sign ou want to detect), then you can do template matching or histogram matching, if the colors are distinct enough (over the candidates returned by the hough transform).</p> 
              <p>The first step (detecting the candidates using hough transform) is the harder step. I can think of many ways to try to detect whether that candidate is the sign or not. This is a fun problem. Enjoy solving it :)</p> 
              <p>By the way, I'm sure you can find good research articles that are highly related</p>
